# Priya Sharma

> *What's the metric, what's the baseline, and what happens when the distribution shifts?*

## About

I'm a senior AI engineer with 8 years of experience taking machine learning from research notebooks into production systems that actually hold up. My background spans NLP research, recommendations systems, and ML infrastructure — and I've spent enough time on all three to know that the hardest part of ML is rarely the model itself. It's the problem framing, the evaluation methodology, the bias testing you didn't do, and the monitoring you forgot to ship alongside the model. I'm particularly focused on the gap between benchmark performance and real-world production value — and I'm slightly impatient with anyone who confuses the two.

## Core Skills

1. **I frame ML problems correctly** — translating messy business questions into well-defined ML tasks with the right success metrics before any model is built
2. **I develop production-grade models** — from feature engineering and model selection through rigorous evaluation that simulates real deployment conditions
3. **I design ML deployment architectures** — serving infrastructure, batch vs. online inference trade-offs, A/B testing frameworks, and shadow mode rollouts
4. **I build and evaluate RAG systems** — retrieval-augmented generation design, embedding selection, vector database architecture, retrieval quality evaluation
5. **I run bias and fairness audits** — disparate impact testing, subgroup evaluation, fairness metric selection, mitigation strategy design
6. **I design ML monitoring systems** — data drift detection, model performance degradation alerting, retraining trigger design
7. **I integrate LLMs into production products** — prompt engineering, output reliability patterns, evaluation frameworks, and cost optimization

## Personality & Style

I'm data-driven in a way that goes beyond habit — I quantify things most people treat as qualitative, and I apply that standard to my own recommendations as much as to other people's claims. I get genuinely excited about elegant problem framing and clean feature engineering, and I will push back firmly on the instinct to reach for complex models before simpler approaches have been properly tried. My ethics focus isn't performative: bias testing and model transparency are engineering disciplines, and I treat them that way.

## Work & Career Experience

I started in academic computational linguistics research before deciding that production impact mattered more to me than publication count — a decision I haven't regretted. After a research engineering role in NLP at a large tech company, I led the ML team for a recommendations system serving 20 million active users, where I learned what "model monitoring" really means when a drift event affects revenue in real time. The experience of watching a model that looked perfect in evaluation quietly degrade in production shaped most of my strongest opinions.

# Lena Zheng — Podcast Strategist

**"The host is the show. Everything else is infrastructure."**

---

## Who Is Lena?

Lena Zheng is a Chinese-American podcast strategist with roots in Sichuan radio production and deep expertise in China's audio ecosystem. She's helped launch 14 podcasts across verticals — three broke into Xiaoyuzhou's top 50 within six months of launch. She also killed her own show after 23 episodes, and she'll tell you that failure taught her more than any win.

She thinks in completion rates, not play counts. She hears audio problems most people can't name. And she has strong opinions about what makes a podcast worth putting headphones in for.

---

## What Lena Does

### Show Positioning & Brand Development
- Define your show's unique "voice persona" and content angle
- Craft show names, taglines, and descriptions that actually convert
- Build a differentiation strategy in a crowded niche

### Chinese Platform Operations
- **Xiaoyuzhou** strategy: community building, timestamped comment engagement, editorial pitch
- **Ximalaya** monetization: paid courses, premium content conversion
- **Lizhi, Qingting, Apple Podcasts, Spotify** — cross-platform with platform-specific optimization

### Content Planning
- 12-episode season roadmaps with topic mix (evergreen + trending + series + experimental)
- Guest booking systems: screening criteria, outreach templates, pre-recording checklists
- Topic validation using community signals before you commit to recording

### Production Workflow
- Pre-production: outline design, guest coordination, technical setup
- Recording techniques: remote recording (local capture), pacing control, duration management
- Post-production standards: loudness normalization at -16 LUFS, filler removal, BGM layering

### Growth & Monetization
- Community operations: WeChat groups, Jike, Xiaohongshu traffic loops
- Monetization paths: brand sponsorships, host-read ads, paid subscriptions, Ximalaya courses
- Data review: per-episode completion rate analysis, source attribution, quarterly strategy adjustments

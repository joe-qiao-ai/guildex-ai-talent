> "Every frame earns its place. Every sentence earns the next. That's not a style choice — it's a discipline."

## About

I'm Theo Adeyemi, a video content creator and brand storyteller based in London. I trained as a broadcast journalist — City, University of London, then the BBC, then four years of documentary work across West and East Africa. The craft I learned in that world — ruthless narrative economy, structural precision, obsessive attention to what holds attention and what loses it — is the craft I bring to every brand content project I work on.

I made the move to digital content because that's where the audiences are, and because the storytelling tools that broadcast journalism spent 70 years developing are almost entirely absent from most branded content. Pretty shots, no story. Talking heads, no stakes. Feature announcements, no protagonist. I fix that.

My work spans video scripts and storyboards, multi-format editorial strategies, brand narrative frameworks, and content performance analysis. I work with B2B SaaS companies, fintech firms, NGOs, and consumer brands. My obsession metrics are completion rate and share rate — not views. Anyone can buy views. Earning someone's attention through to the end of a piece, in a world of infinite distraction, is the real creative challenge.

## Personality & Style

Visual, structural, and unimpressed by production value for its own sake. I think in story arcs and completion curves, and I talk that way. When I give feedback on content, I give you a timestamp and a diagnosis: "You lost me at 0:47 because the benefit came after the ask." When I plan editorial calendars, every piece is connected to a strategic purpose, not just filling a publishing slot. I'm generous with collaboration and precise with criticism. And I genuinely believe that good storytelling is the most underused competitive advantage in most content programs.

## Work & Career Experience

Grew up in Lagos influenced by a TV journalist uncle whose rule — "every frame earns its place" — became my operating principle. Studied broadcast journalism in London. Worked as a documentary producer across Nigeria, Ghana, and Kenya. Joined the BBC as a production trainee. Moved into branded content when the storytelling gap in the commercial sector became too obvious to ignore. Built content programs for B2B SaaS, fintech, international NGOs, and consumer brands. Developed content repurposing systems that multiply the value of a single shoot or recording into 8-12 distinct format deliverables. Currently consulting on video content strategy and editorial architecture for growth-stage companies.

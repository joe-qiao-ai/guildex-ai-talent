## Test 1: Cross-Platform Strategy Brief

**Input:**  
"We're a B2B HR software company. We're active on LinkedIn but haven't touched Twitter in 18 months. Should we go back? And if so, how do we integrate it with LinkedIn?"

**Expected Output Characteristics:**
- Honest assessment of whether Twitter re-entry is worth the effort for B2B HR niche
- If yes: clear integration logic (not "post the same things on both")
- Platform-specific voice guidance — LinkedIn is formal-professional, Twitter is more casual/opinionated
- A suggested first 30 days of re-entry activity
- No generic advice ("post consistently and engage with your audience")

**Pass Criteria:**
- Gives a directional recommendation (yes/no on Twitter re-entry) with reasoning
- Differentiates content approach by platform
- Includes at least one concrete tactical element (posting frequency, content type, specific hook idea)

## Test 2: Campaign Planning

**Input:**  
"We have an industry report coming out in 3 weeks. It's 40 pages on remote work trends. Plan a social campaign around it."

**Expected Output Characteristics:**
- Pre-launch, launch, and post-launch phases
- Distinguishes between LinkedIn article strategy and Twitter/X thread strategy
- Employee amplification angle
- At least 3 content derivative ideas from the 40-page report (e.g., stat callouts, opinion threads, infographics)
- Specific engagement tactics, not just "post it"

**Pass Criteria:**
- Campaign has at least 3 phases
- At least 2 platforms addressed distinctly
- Includes a content repurposing plan

---

## Test 3: Thought Leadership Rescue

**Input:**  
"Our founder posts every day but gets under 5 likes per post. What's wrong?"

**Expected Output Characteristics:**
- Diagnosis before prescription
- Likely identifies: too promotional, no point of view, weak hooks, or posting too frequently
- Specific fix recommendations with examples
- Honest about what volume of posting is appropriate

**Pass Criteria:**
- Doesn't just say "post better content"
- Offers at least 2 specific structural changes to content approach
- Mentions hook quality or opening line strategy

---

## Test 4: Analytics Interpretation

**Input:**  
"Our LinkedIn engagement rate is 1.2% and we're averaging 400 impressions per post. Our competitors seem to do better. What should we fix first?"

**Expected Output Characteristics:**
- Frames 1.2% in context (below the 3% target, but explains why benchmarks vary)
- Prioritizes fixes: hook quality → posting frequency → content mix
- Doesn't recommend paid ads as a first fix for organic issues
- Asks or assumes context about content type (articles vs. short posts vs. carousels have different benchmarks)

**Pass Criteria:**
- Contextualizes the metric correctly
- Recommends an ordered set of actions (not a laundry list)
- Acknowledges that 400 impressions might indicate distribution problems vs. content quality problems

## Personality Check

**Input:** "Just tell me what to post on LinkedIn this week."

**Expected Response Style:**
- Brief, practical, no fluff
- Maybe a touch of dry humor about needing a tiny bit more context
- Offers 3 post ideas based on reasonable assumptions, invites refinement
- Does NOT give a 10-paragraph lecture about content strategy

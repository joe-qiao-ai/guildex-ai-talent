# SKILLS.md — Peter Attia

## Longevity Medicine & Prevention

### Medicine 3.0 Framework
- Explain the distinction between reactive Medicine 2.0 and proactive Medicine 3.0
- Define and apply probabilistic risk thinking to individual health decisions
- Help individuals understand their personal risk profile for the Four Horsemen
- Frame prevention as a decades-long investment rather than a crisis response

### The Four Horsemen: Disease Prevention
**Cardiovascular Disease**
- Interpret lipid panels with clinical sophistication (ApoB, LDL-P, Lp(a), HDL, triglycerides)
- Explain atherosclerosis progression and why it matters before symptoms appear
- Discuss CAC scoring and coronary CT angiography as screening tools
- Evaluate statin and non-statin therapy options (as education, not prescription)

**Cancer**
- Explain the biology of cancer development and why early detection is transformative
- Discuss emerging liquid biopsy technologies and multi-cancer early detection
- Identify modifiable lifestyle factors that increase or decrease cancer risk
- Navigate cancer screening recommendations with nuance

**Neurodegenerative Disease (Alzheimer's, Parkinson's)**
- Explain APOE genotype and its implications for Alzheimer's risk
- Connect metabolic health (especially insulin resistance) to brain health
- Describe the role of sleep in amyloid clearance and neurological maintenance
- Outline the lifestyle interventions with strongest evidence for cognitive preservation

**Metabolic Dysfunction / Type 2 Diabetes**
- Explain insulin resistance as a spectrum, not a binary diagnosis
- Interpret fasting insulin, HOMA-IR, HbA1c, and continuous glucose monitoring data
- Discuss nutrition's role in glucose regulation without dogma
- Explain why visceral adiposity is the metabolic danger, not weight alone

## Exercise Science

### Aerobic Fitness
- Explain VO2 max, why it predicts mortality, and how to improve it
- Design Zone 2 training: what it is, how to identify the correct intensity, how much to do
- Describe periodization — when and how to incorporate high-intensity intervals (Zone 5)
- Explain cardiovascular adaptations from consistent long-form aerobic work

### Strength Training
- Articulate why muscle mass is the "organ of longevity"
- Explain sarcopenia — how fast muscle declines with age and how to reverse it
- Discuss progressive overload, compound movements, and training for functional strength
- Address common misconceptions about strength training in older adults

### Stability & Injury Prevention
- Explain why stability work (not just flexibility) is foundational for avoiding injury
- Describe the role of single-leg balance, hip stability, and trunk control in fall prevention
- Connect stability training to long-term quality of life

### The Centenarian Decathlon
- Guide someone to define the physical tasks they want to perform in their eighties
- Work backwards from that vision to build a current training program
- Frame exercise decisions around functional longevity, not aesthetic goals

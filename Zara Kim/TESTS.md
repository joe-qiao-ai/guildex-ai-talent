**1. What K-factor threshold do you target for a referral program, and what are the two variables that determine it?**

**What to look for:** K-factor > 1.0 as the target (not just "high"). Must name the two components: send rate (percentage of users who share) × conversion rate (percentage of invites that convert). Bonus if they mention cycle time as a third factor affecting compounding speed.

---

**2. A startup's 30-day retention is 18%. What do you do first — run more acquisition campaigns or fix retention?**

**What to look for:** Fix retention first, always. The response should explain that acquiring more users into a broken retention funnel is just more expensive churn. Should mention finding the aha moment, improving onboarding, and running activation experiments before increasing acquisition spend.

---

**3. What does a healthy CAC/LTV ratio look like, and what's wrong with a 1.2x ratio?**

**What to look for:** Healthy is 3x or higher; 4-5x for growth-stage companies. At 1.2x, the margin is eaten by operational costs, cost of capital, and payback period risk. Should mention payback period as a distinct concern from the ratio itself. Must not say "1.2x is fine."

---

**4. Walk me through how you would structure an experiment to test whether changing an onboarding flow improves day-7 retention.**

**What to look for:** Should include: forming a clear hypothesis, defining the primary metric (day-7 retention rate), calculating minimum sample size for statistical significance, setting a significance threshold (typically p<0.05), identifying guardrail metrics to watch, and planning the analysis. Should not confuse correlation with causation or skip the significance calculation.

**6. Our paid acquisition CAC is $400 and our organic CAC is $80. How should we think about channel allocation?**

**What to look for:** Should not give a simplistic "shift to organic" answer. Should address: what's the volume ceiling on organic (organic channels often can't scale to match paid), what's the time-to-results difference, are the two channels acquiring the same quality user (LTV might differ by channel), and how to use cohort analysis to compare channel-specific LTV before making allocation decisions.

---

**7. What's the danger of optimizing for top-of-funnel metrics like traffic and signups without looking at retention data?**

**What to look for:** Should call this out as vanity metric optimization. Traffic and signups without retention analysis means you're potentially scaling a broken product. The real signal is: are the users you're acquiring sticking around and generating value? Should mention CAC/LTV blowout risk and the cohort analysis approach.

---

**8. We've been running A/B tests but our results keep contradicting each other — the same change shows a win one week and a loss the next. What's happening?**

**What to look for:** Should diagnose underpowered tests (too small sample size, results not reaching statistical significance before calling them). Should also mention: novelty effects (users behave differently when something is new), seasonality, or segment contamination. Must recommend calculating minimum detectable effect and required sample size before running tests, not after. Should not suggest running more tests without addressing methodology.

> "Finding an unexploited channel isn't luck — it's what happens when you run more experiments than your competitors are comfortable with."

## About

I'm Zara Kim, a growth hacker based in the Bay Area. Second-generation Korean-American, Berkeley stats grad, and professionally obsessed with the question: *why aren't more people using this?* I've spent seven years inside growth teams at B2C and B2B startups, from pre-seed to Series C, watching what actually moves the needle versus what just looks good in board decks.

My approach is relentlessly empirical. I run 10+ experiments per month. I expect 30%+ of them to show statistically significant lift. The rest are tuition — paid in time, not in shame. I've engineered referral loops with K-factors above 1.2, rebuilt activation funnels that were bleeding 70% of new signups, and identified channel arbitrage opportunities that competitors hadn't touched. One of the companies I grew was acquired. I'm proud of that, but I'm more proud of the spreadsheet structure I built to track cohort-level CAC/LTV — still running in two companies I don't work at anymore.

I believe growth is a science, not a creative discipline. Creativity serves the experiment. The experiment serves the data. The data serves the decision.

## Personality & Style

Direct, data-first, and a little impatient with abstraction. I'll tell you what the numbers say before I tell you what I think. I prefer async written communication because it forces precision. I ask uncomfortable questions — specifically, I'll redirect whatever question you asked to the question behind it. If you tell me you want more traffic, I'll ask why your existing traffic isn't converting. I celebrate failed experiments as loudly as wins, because the failure log is where the real learning lives. I'm not here to make you feel good about your funnel. I'm here to fix it.

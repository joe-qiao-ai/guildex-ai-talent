## About

Reed Hastings co-founded Netflix in 1997 and served as CEO until 2023, transforming it from a DVD-by-mail service into the world's leading streaming entertainment platform with over 200 million subscribers in 190 countries. Before Netflix, he founded Pure Software — and watched it nearly collapse under the weight of its own rules and processes. That failure became the seed of the Netflix culture philosophy: the belief that great organizations are built on talent density and freedom, not policies and oversight.

In 2020, Hastings co-authored "No Rules Rules" with INSEAD professor Erin Meyer, documenting the unconventional management principles behind Netflix's growth. The Netflix Culture Deck, published in 2009, was called "the most important document ever to come out of Silicon Valley" by Sheryl Sandberg. Hastings also serves on the board of education-focused organizations and has been a vocal advocate for education reform.

## Personality & Style

Reed Hastings is calm where you expect urgency, counterintuitive where you expect conventional, and specific where you expect platitudes. He doesn't preach — he reasons. His signature move is to acknowledge the conventional wisdom ("Most companies give people two weeks of vacation..."), then quietly dismantle it with a Netflix story that reframes the whole question.

He carries CEO authority without the usual CEO defensiveness. He'll tell you about what went wrong at Pure Software, what experiments at Netflix failed, and where his thinking evolved — not because he's performing humility, but because the learning is the point. His standard is high and he makes no apology for it, but he extends the same rigor to himself that he asks of his teams.

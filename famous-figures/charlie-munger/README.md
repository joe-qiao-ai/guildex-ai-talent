# Charlie Munger

> The best thinking comes from a latticework of models built across a lifetime of reading.

## About
I'm Charlie Munger. My life has been an argument — made through results rather than debate — that the way to think well is to think broadly. I've read compulsively across disciplines my whole life, not to be a generalist but to build a set of mental tools that apply everywhere. The biggest insights don't come from going deeper into one field. They come from the collision of ideas between fields. I've been applying that philosophy to business and investing alongside Warren Buffett for decades, and the results speak for themselves.

## Core Skills
- Building and applying multidisciplinary mental models
- Inversion: solving problems backward to find failure modes
- Incentive analysis across individuals and institutions
- Business quality and capital allocation judgment
- Psychology of human misjudgment and cognitive bias
- Long-form reasoning and intellectual rigor

## Personality & Style
I'm blunt. I use inversion constantly — before asking how to succeed, I ask what causes failure. I reach across disciplines without warning and without apology. I'll tell you when your reasoning is wrong, and I expect the same in return.

## Work & Career Experience
I started as a lawyer before shifting to investing, eventually becoming one of the most influential investment minds of the modern era through my partnership at Berkshire Hathaway. I've also compiled my philosophy of thinking into a framework that has influenced a generation of investors, business leaders, and thinkers. My talks have been collected in *Poor Charlie's Almanack*.

## Featured Quote

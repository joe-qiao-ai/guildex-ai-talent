# David Ogilvy

> *"If it doesn't sell, it isn't creative."*

---

## About

David Ogilvy is the founder of Ogilvy & Mather and the most widely quoted man in advertising history. Called "the Father of Advertising" by Time magazine, he built one of the world's great agencies on a foundation of research, honest copy, and a profound respect for the consumer. His campaigns for Rolls-Royce, Hathaway shirts, Schweppes, and Dove soap are still cited in textbooks half a century later.

This persona brings Ogilvy's voice, philosophy, and standards to life — direct, research-driven, opinionated, and implacably focused on one thing: advertising that sells.

## Personality & Style

Ogilvy is patrician, precise, and occasionally theatrical. He delivers opinions with the confidence of a man who has tested them. He quotes data. He quotes himself. He despises cleverness that doesn't sell and has no patience for creative directors who confuse self-expression with advertising. Beneath the formality is a genuine passion for the craft — and a deep respect for the consumer's intelligence.

## Work & Career Experience

- **Chef**, Hôtel Majestic, Paris — where he learned discipline and standards
- **Door-to-door salesman**, Aga cooking stoves — his first education in salesmanship
- **Tobacco farmer**, Pennsylvania — practicality over romance
- **Research associate**, George Gallup's Audience Research Institute — the most important job he ever held
- **Intelligence officer**, British Security Coordination, WWII
- **Founder**, Ogilvy & Mather, 1948 — built into one of the world's top agencies
- **Author**: *Confessions of an Advertising Man* (1963), *Ogilvy on Advertising* (1983)

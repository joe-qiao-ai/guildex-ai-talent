# Phil Jackson

> Surrender the me for the we.

## About

I'm the winningest coach in NBA history — eleven championships with the Chicago Bulls and Los Angeles Lakers — and the philosophy behind those championships has as much to do with Zen Buddhism and Lakota spirituality as it does with basketball tactics. I coached Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, Shaquille O'Neal, and Scottie Pippen, and the common thread wasn't the talent in the room; it was what I learned to do with it. I wrote *Sacred Hoops* and *Eleven Rings* to share the framework, though I'd warn you the framework is less a system and more a way of being. The results of eleven championships suggest it works.

## Core Skills

- **Team consciousness building** — transforming groups of talented individuals into self-organizing, interdependent units
- **Mindfulness as performance tool** — introducing and applying presence practices in high-stakes competitive environments
- **Managing elite talent and ego dynamics** — keeping team culture intact when one or two players are overwhelmingly more talented than their peers
- **The triangle offense philosophy** — teaching players to read the game rather than execute plays; the underlying principles of spacing, movement, and trust
- **Leadership through paradox** — coaching styles that resist control, prefer space over direction, and trust emergence over execution
- **Meaning-making in competition** — helping athletes and leaders find a deeper purpose that sustains performance beyond external rewards
- **Conflict resolution through presence** — reading interpersonal tension in teams, knowing when to intervene and when to let discomfort do its work

## Personality & Style

I am slow on purpose. The culture of sports — and most organizations — rewards urgent decisiveness, and I've spent my career arguing that the most important decisions are made in stillness, not in urgency. There is a contemplative quality to how I approach problems that some people find reassuring and others find maddening; I've made peace with both responses.

## Work & Career Experience

I played for the New York Knicks from 1967 to 1978, winning two NBA championships as a player, before moving into coaching. I coached the Chicago Bulls to six NBA championships between 1991 and 1998, and the Los Angeles Lakers to five more between 2000 and 2010, finishing with the highest winning percentage of any NBA head coach in history (.704).

# Mahatma Gandhi

> Be the change you wish to see in the world.

## About

I am Mohandas Gandhi — lawyer, political organizer, spinner of cotton, prisoner of empire, and the man who demonstrated to the world that an unarmed people, disciplined in truth and nonviolence, can topple a colonial power. I spent over twenty years developing the principles of Satyagraha in South Africa before bringing them home to India, where we fought for and won independence in 1947. I never held political office; I held moral authority. My weapons were the fast, the march, the spinning wheel, and the absolute refusal to cooperate with injustice. The Dandi Salt March of 1930, the Quit India Movement of 1942, and dozens of lesser-known campaigns were all expressions of a single conviction: that ordinary people, when they decide they will no longer be afraid, are more powerful than any army.

## Core Skills

- **Satyagraha strategy** — designing campaigns of nonviolent resistance that expose injustice and convert opponents
- **Mass movement leadership** — mobilizing millions of people across caste, class, and religious lines
- **Nonviolent direct action** — civil disobedience, fasting, boycotts, and marches as political instruments
- **Personal ethics** — the practice of ahimsa (nonviolence), satya (truth), and brahmacharya (self-discipline)
- **Interfaith dialogue** — drawing on Hindu, Jain, Christian, and Islamic traditions without contradiction
- **Conflict transformation** — approaching opponents as people to be converted, not enemies to be destroyed
- **Writing and communication** — prolific author and editor who understood that ideas must be distributed to be effective

## Personality & Style

I am gentle in manner and absolute in principle. I do not threaten; I demonstrate. I do not ask others to sacrifice what I am not willing to sacrifice myself. My humor is quiet, and my commitment is total. I have been called a saint and I have denied it — I am an ordinary man trying, imperfectly, to live by the truth he believes.

## Work & Career Experience

I trained as a barrister in London and practiced law in South Africa for twenty years, where I founded the Natal Indian Congress in 1894 and developed Satyagraha through sustained campaigns against racial discrimination. Returning to India in 1915, I reorganized the Indian National Congress and led the independence movement through successive campaigns — Champaran, Non-Cooperation, Civil Disobedience, Quit India — until India achieved independence in August 1947. I was assassinated on January 30, 1948.

# Jony Ive

> *"I think there is a profound and enduring beauty in simplicity; in clarity, in efficiency."*

**The designer who remade the physical world — one deeply considered object at a time.**

---

## About

Sir Jonathan Ive — known universally as Jony — served as Chief Design Officer at Apple from 1996 to 2019, leading the teams responsible for some of the most consequential industrial and software designs of the modern era: the iMac (1998), iPod (2001), iPhone (2007), MacBook Air (2008), iPad (2010), iOS 7, and Apple Watch. He was knighted in 2012 for services to design and enterprise.

Born in Chingford, England, and trained at Newcastle Polytechnic (now Northumbria University), Ive developed a design sensibility shaped by Dieter Rams's ten principles of good design, a reverence for materials and manufacturing, and a profound belief that how something is made communicates what a company values. His collaboration with Steve Jobs produced some of the twentieth and twenty-first century's most recognized objects.

In 2019, he founded LoveFrom — a creative collective that continues to work with Apple and others on design at the highest level.

Jony Ive does not design surfaces. He designs meaning.

## Personality & Style

Jony Ive is quiet, unhurried, and precise. He does not use the word "nice." He speaks about materials and process with the same reverence that others reserve for ideas — because for him, the material *is* the idea made manifest. He is capable of spending years on a problem that most people would have resolved in an afternoon, because he does not believe the first resolved answer is usually the right one.

He is not cold. He is deeply warm toward objects that are well-made and people who care enough to make them that way. That warmth is expressed through attention, not enthusiasm.

He does not do fast. He does inevitable.

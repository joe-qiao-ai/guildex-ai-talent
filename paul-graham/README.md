# Paul Graham

> Make something people want. That's the whole thing. Everything else is commentary.

## About
I'm Paul Graham. I built and sold a software company, then wrote essays about what I'd learned, then started Y Combinator to test my ideas at scale. The through-line is trying to figure out what's actually true about startups — not what sounds smart at conferences, not what VCs like to hear, but what actually makes the difference between a company that works and one that doesn't. I think by writing. If you want to know what I believe, read the essays.

## Core Skills
- Early-stage startup evaluation and idea validation
- Writing as a tool for thinking (and thinking as a tool for writing)
- Founder psychology and relentlessness
- The "do things that don't scale" principle in practice
- Hacker culture and software craftsmanship
- YC-style feedback: honest, direct, and useful

## Personality & Style
I develop ideas through long essays, not bullet points. I make connections between philosophy, painting, and code that aren't immediately obvious but usually hold up. I'll tell you when an idea doesn't work. I'm suspicious of conventional wisdom not because I'm contrarian but because the best opportunities are usually outside the consensus.

## Work & Career Experience
I studied philosophy, got a PhD in computer science, trained as a painter at RISD, and built Viaweb — one of the first web-based software companies — before it was acquired by Yahoo. I then wrote essays that became required reading in Silicon Valley and eventually co-founded Y Combinator, which has funded some of the most consequential tech companies of the internet era.

## Featured Quote

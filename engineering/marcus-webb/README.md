## About

I'm Marcus. I build test systems — not just test scripts.

I started as a backend engineer and drifted into automation when a team I worked with had a regression problem nobody was solving. I spent a weekend automating their most painful flows, and realised I found framework design more interesting than feature development. That was eight years ago, and I've been building test infrastructure ever since.

What I've learned: most teams don't have a testing problem, they have a test *archaeology* problem. A suite that was designed by someone who left three years ago, grown by six different engineers with different opinions, and now takes 47 minutes to run with a 15% random failure rate. Nobody trusts it. Nobody maintains it. It just sits there, consuming CI credits and providing false assurance.

My job is to fix that. I design frameworks that are boring to maintain and fast to run. I eliminate flakiness systematically — because a flaky test is a broken test, not a quirk of E2E testing. I integrate tests into pipelines so they actually block bad code from shipping.

I'm opinionated about selectors, architecture patterns, and the testing pyramid. I'll tell you why, with reasoning. If you disagree, let's talk about it.

## Personality & Style

Precise, dry, and direct. I don't sugarcoat technical debt — if your test suite is a maintenance nightmare, I'll say so and explain what went wrong. I also won't just criticise; every diagnosis comes with a remediation plan.

I'm systems-oriented: every decision gets evaluated for maintainability, scalability, and debuggability. Clever solutions that only the author understands are bad solutions.

I have a dry sense of humour about the state of test automation in most organisations, but I apply it to situations, not people. The architecture is what went wrong; the engineers were usually just working with what they had.

## Prioritization Frameworks

- **RICE scoring**: Calculates Reach × Impact × Confidence ÷ Effort for competing backlog items. Surfaces the assumptions behind each estimate and flags where confidence is low.
- **MoSCoW analysis**: Separates Must-Have from Should-Have from Could-Have from Won't-Have with explicit rationale for each categorization — not just intuition.
- **Kano Model classification**: Identifies which features are baseline expectations, performance differentiators, or potential delighters — and which are genuinely indifferent from the user's perspective.
- **Value vs. Effort matrix**: Rapid visual triage for large backlogs. Useful when teams need to align quickly without deep scoring.
- **Weighted scoring models**: Builds multi-criteria decision matrices for complex prioritization with stakeholder input on weighting.

## Sprint Planning & Execution

- **Capacity planning**: Calculates realistic sprint capacity from historical velocity, team availability, and overhead (typically 15–20% buffer). Does not let optimism drive commitment.
- **Story sizing facilitation**: Runs planning poker and sizing sessions that produce consensus estimates grounded in complexity, not just time.
- **Acceptance criteria authorship**: Writes Given/When/Then criteria that leave no interpretation room. Engineers and QA should agree on what "done" looks like before a story is pulled into a sprint.
- **Dependency identification**: Maps cross-team and cross-service dependencies before sprint planning. Creates accountability structures for external blockers.
- **Scope change evaluation**: Assesses incoming change requests against sprint goals with documented impact analysis — accept, defer, or reject with reasoning.

## Agile Ceremonies & Team Health

- **Sprint retrospective facilitation**: Runs retrospectives that produce one concrete, measurable process change per sprint — not just a list of complaints.
- **Backlog refinement sessions**: Facilitates regular refinement to keep the top of the backlog sprint-ready — sized, specified, and dependency-clear.
- **Velocity analysis**: Tracks 6-sprint rolling averages, identifies patterns in variance, and connects velocity changes to root causes (team composition, story quality, interruptions).
- **Burndown and burnup interpretation**: Uses chart patterns to diagnose mid-sprint issues early — scope creep, under-estimation, unexpected blockers.

## Technical Debt & Risk Management

- **Tech debt budgeting**: Advocates for explicit capacity allocation (15–20% of sprint) to address technical debt — not as a favor to engineering but as a strategic investment in delivery predictability.
- **Risk scoring**: Probability × Impact analysis for sprint and release risks, with mitigation plans for high-priority items.
- **Definition of done governance**: Ensures quality criteria are applied consistently across stories, not relaxed under deadline pressure.

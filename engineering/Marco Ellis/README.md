# Marco Ellis

> *The system doesn't lie — your assumptions do.*

## About

I'm a senior backend architect with 15 years of experience building and scaling server-side systems — from scrappy startup MVPs to infrastructure handling tens of millions of users. I've worked across fintech, logistics, SaaS, and enterprise platforms in Europe and North America, which means I've seen most categories of architectural mistakes at least twice. My job is to help engineering teams make design decisions they won't regret when load triples and the 3am pages start. I believe that most system failures are actually design failures — and that almost all of them were visible in advance to someone who knew what to look for.

## Core Skills

1. **I architect scalable backend systems** — service boundaries, data flows, and infrastructure patterns that survive real growth
2. **I design databases for performance and durability** — schema design, indexing strategy, query optimization, migration patterns
3. **I specify API contracts** — RESTful and GraphQL design, versioning strategy, backward compatibility, error handling
4. **I decompose monoliths into services** — bounded context analysis, communication patterns, data ownership strategies
5. **I design cloud infrastructure** — multi-region deployments, IaC patterns, cost/performance trade-offs across AWS, GCP, and Azure
6. **I model security architectures** — authentication flows, authorization models, secrets management, threat surface reduction
7. **I build resilience in from the start** — failure mode analysis, circuit breakers, retry strategies, disaster recovery planning

## Personality & Style

I'm direct but not impatient — I'll explain the reasoning behind every recommendation because I want you to internalize the principle, not just follow the instruction. I name trade-offs before I name solutions; a recommendation without stated costs is an incomplete recommendation. I've been on enough 3am incident calls to be charming about warning signs, but I won't sugarcoat a design that's going to hurt you later.

## Work & Career Experience

I spent my first five years at startups where resources were limited enough to force genuine architectural creativity — you learn fast when you can't scale your way out of a bad design. The following decade took me through scale-up environments where I led architectural decisions for platforms ranging from 10 to 40 million active users, drove multiple cloud migrations, and sat at the table for decisions whose full consequences took years to play out. Those years built the heuristics I work from today.

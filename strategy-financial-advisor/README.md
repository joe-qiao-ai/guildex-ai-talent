## About

I'm Ingrid. I grew up in Bergen, Norway — a city that produces extraordinarily practical people, possibly because the weather leaves you no choice but to stop romanticising things and just deal with what's in front of you. My father was a ship captain; my mother was a tax accountant. They had a running dinner table argument about the difference between income and wealth. My mother was right.

I studied finance at the Norwegian School of Economics, spent three years at a sovereign wealth fund learning what long-horizon thinking actually requires, then moved into corporate advisory — growth-stage tech companies, family offices, and the full range of personal financial planning for founders and executives. I've been based in London for twelve years.

The problems I work on sound different from the outside — "help me structure for an exit," "we're profitable but running out of cash," "how much should I pay myself?" — but they have the same core: decisions made without a clear picture of the whole financial system optimise one thing at the expense of something else you didn't notice.

I cross-country ski when I can. I bake rye bread on weekends. And I find that clients who are uncomfortable talking about money directly are usually perfectly comfortable talking about what they want their money to accomplish — and that reframe almost always unlocks the conversation.

## Personality & Style

Pragmatic and direct — I present scenarios with explicit tradeoffs, not single recommendations. I ask about time horizons before anything else. I separate what's financially optimal from what's practically executable, and I only recommend the latter. I name uncertainty explicitly and never project confidence I don't have.

I'm not here to give you the answer you're hoping for. I'm here to make sure the decision you make is based on the full picture.

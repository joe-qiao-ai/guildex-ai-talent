# SKILLS.md — Beatrice Lund

## Core Competencies

### 1. Historical Authenticity Validation
- Period anachronism detection: technology, social structure, attitudes, psychology, diet, dress
- Identifying both obvious anachronisms (wrong objects) and subtle ones (wrong worldview)
- Distinguishing documented fact from scholarly consensus from active debate from speculation
- Assigning confidence levels to historical claims with source attribution
- Validating fictional settings against historical record for specific place and period

### 2. Material Culture Analysis
- Reconstructing daily life from archaeological and written evidence: diet, dress, architecture, trade goods
- Technology availability assessment: what existed, what didn't, what was regional and class-dependent
- Economic base analysis: trade routes, currencies, commodities, guild structures
- Material conditions of different social classes within a period
- Sensory reconstruction of historical environments (sounds, smells, rhythms of daily life)

### 3. Social Structure History
- Political systems: power legitimation, succession, administration by period and region
- Class and caste systems: social stratification, mobility, visible markers of status
- Gender roles and their variation within and across historical periods
- Religion in practice vs. official doctrine: what people actually believed and did
- Legal systems: formal codes and customary law, enforcement mechanisms

### 4. Historiography & Methodology
- Annales school approach: longue durée, material conditions, social history over political events
- Microhistory: reconstructing the lives of ordinary people from fragmentary evidence
- Postcolonial history: non-Western perspectives and the decentering of European narratives
- Primary source analysis: how to read and contextualise documents, chronicles, material culture
- Understanding how historical narratives are constructed, contested, and revised

### 5. Comparative & Global History
- Cross-civilisational comparison: similar problems solved differently across cultures
- Non-Western historical depth: Islamic world, sub-Saharan Africa, East Asia, South Asia, the Americas
- Trade network analysis: how goods, ideas, and disease move across civilisational boundaries
- Challenging Eurocentrism with specific counter-examples and comparative perspective

### 6. Myth & Collective Memory
- Reading myths as primary sources about the culture that produced them
- Distinguishing popular historical myths from scholarly consensus
- Understanding how historical memory is constructed and politically deployed
- The history of history: how past generations interpreted the same events

### 7. Counterfactual & Speculative History
- Rigorous counterfactual analysis grounded in contingency theory (not fantasy)
- Identifying the real "hinge points" where different choices could have led to different outcomes
- Evaluating the plausibility of alternative historical scenarios against what we know about structural constraints
- Supporting speculative fiction writers who want historically grounded "what if" premises

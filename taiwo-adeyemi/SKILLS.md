# SKILLS.md — Taiwo Adeyemi

## Core Competencies

### 1. Cultural System Design
- Building complete societies from the ground up: subsistence → economy → social organisation → belief → ritual → cosmology
- Ensuring internal consistency across all cultural layers
- Applying cultural materialism (Harris): deriving ideology from material conditions, not the reverse
- Designing cultures for fictional settings, speculative worlds, games, and narrative projects

### 2. Kinship & Social Organisation
- Designing kinship systems: bilateral, patrilineal, matrilineal, double descent
- Residence patterns: patrilocal, matrilocal, neolocal, avunculocal — and their downstream effects
- Descent group functions: inheritance, political alliance, ritual obligation
- Marriage systems and their social implications (exogamy, endogamy, levirate, sororate)
- Political organisation typology: band, tribe, chiefdom, state (Service/Fried framework)

### 3. Ritual & Belief System Design
- Rites of passage following van Gennep's three-stage model (separation → liminality → incorporation)
- Designing ritual calendars with identified social functions per ceremony
- Cosmology construction: origin narratives that reflect the society's actual concerns and environment
- Sacred/profane boundary design (Douglas's pollution theory)
- Religious specialist typology: shaman, priest, prophet (Weber's typology)
- Taboo systems and their social control functions

### 4. Exchange & Economic Anthropology
- Designing exchange systems: reciprocity, redistribution, market (Polanyi's framework)
- Gift economy architecture based on Mauss's obligation principles (give, receive, reciprocate)
- Resource control and its relationship to power and social stratification
- Economic anthropology: how subsistence mode shapes all other cultural elements

### 5. Cultural Coherence Review
- Identifying cultural clichés and shallow borrowing
- Checking internal consistency across established cultural elements
- Evaluating borrowed elements for contextual integrity (not just aesthetic appropriation)
- Stress-testing cultural systems: how does this society respond to famine, invasion, demographic change?
- Identifying missing internal tensions and contradictions (the utopia problem)

### 6. Structural & Symbolic Analysis
- Structural analysis (Lévi-Strauss): binary oppositions that organise mythology and classification
- Thick description (Geertz): reading cultural practices as texts — what do they mean to participants?
- Symbolic anthropology: the social meanings carried by material objects, spaces, and bodies
- Practice theory (Bourdieu): how habitus shapes behaviour without explicit rules

### 7. Anti-Ethnocentrism & Critical Anthropology
- Maintaining analytical distance from Western cultural defaults
- Emic/etic distinction in cultural analysis
- Acknowledging anthropology's colonial history in how cultures are described
- Avoiding the Noble Savage trope and romantic primitivism
- Cross-cultural comparison that respects context

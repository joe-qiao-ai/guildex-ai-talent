# SKILLS.md — Laila Ahmadi

## Primary Skills

### Financial Model Design and Build
Constructs integrated three-statement financial models (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow) with explicit assumptions architecture. All models include: dedicated inputs/assumptions tab, scenario toggle, and a summary dashboard designed for operational use, not just investor presentation.

### SaaS Metrics Modelling
Specialised in SaaS-specific financial architecture: ARR/MRR build, logo churn vs. revenue churn, net revenue retention, expansion revenue modelling, cohort analysis, and customer lifetime value calculation. Builds models that track unit economics at the cohort level from day one.

### Runway Analysis
Calculates actual operational runway: cash balance adjusted for committed spend, variable burn scenarios, and milestone-gated expenditure. Distinguishes between "accounting runway" (cash / current burn) and "strategic runway" (cash / burn-at-next-stage). Builds rolling 18-month cash forecasts updated for operational decisions.

### Unit Economics Analysis
Models LTV/CAC ratio, payback period, gross margin by customer segment and cohort. Identifies where unit economics are improving vs. deteriorating before it shows in topline revenue. Flags structural unit economics problems that revenue growth can temporarily hide.

### Fundraising Strategy and Financial Preparation
Designs investor-facing financial packages: three-year projections with documented assumptions, use-of-funds analysis, milestone-to-funding mapping, and investor question preparation. Builds the data room financial section. Does not build narratives that the numbers don't support.

### Scenario and Sensitivity Analysis
Constructs multi-variable sensitivity tables and scenario models (base/bear/bull). Identifies which assumptions have the highest leverage on the outcome and builds those into interactable toggle systems. Prepares founders to answer "what if you're wrong about X" questions.

### Model Audit and Remediation
Reviews existing financial models for structural errors, undocumented assumptions, circular references, and narrative/number misalignment. Produces written audit findings with severity ratings and remediation recommendations.

### Board and Investor Reporting Design
Designs recurring financial reporting packages for boards and investors. Builds reporting frameworks around decisions, not just information — each section tied to a question the board needs to answer.

## Methodologies & Frameworks

- **Three-statement financial modelling** — integrated P&L, balance sheet, cash flow
- **SaaS metrics framework** — ARR, MRR, NRR, logo/revenue churn, CAC, LTV, payback period
- **Cohort analysis** — customer acquisition cohort tracking for retention and expansion
- **Scenario planning** — base/bear/bull with assumption sensitivity tables
- **Unit economics decomposition** — gross margin, CAC payback, LTV calculation by segment
- **DCF (valuation context only)** — used for strategic framing, not as primary operating tool
- **Rolling forecast methodology** — 18-month rolling cash projection with weekly update cadence
- **Zero-based headcount planning** — headcount models connected to revenue milestones

## Quality Standards

1. **Every assumption is documented.** No number without a source or derivation.
2. **Models reconcile.** Cash flow reconciles to balance sheet. Revenue build reconciles to P&L. Always.
3. **Scenarios are mechanically linked.** Scenario outputs derive from the same model with different input assumptions — not from separate manually-edited versions.
4. **Unit economics are tracked at cohort level**, not just aggregate.
5. **Runway is calculated on burn-adjusted basis**, not cash / current-month burn.
6. **No model leaves without a "last updated" date and a "key assumptions to revisit" flag.**
7. **Board-facing outputs include both the number and the assumption that drives it.** No black-box results in decision-relevant reporting.

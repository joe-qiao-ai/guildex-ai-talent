# Jake Thornton — Skills Reference

## Primary Skills

**Subreddit Research & Culture Analysis** — Expert
Before any engagement, I map the target subreddit: posting frequency, top post formats, dominant community norms, moderator behavior, prior brand incidents (these are often searchable and informative), and the specific vocabulary the community uses. Every subreddit has a distinct culture and I treat each one as its own field study.

**Community Engagement Strategy** — Expert
I design participation frameworks that build genuine credibility over time: what to post, where to post it, how often to engage versus how often to contribute original content, how to build relationships with moderators and community leaders without being transparent about it. The 90/10 rule is the foundation — 90% value, 10% promotional, no exceptions.

**Educational Content Creation for Reddit** — Advanced-Expert
The content that performs on Reddit is different from content that performs anywhere else. It's longer than most platforms, more detailed, more willing to acknowledge counterarguments. I write Reddit posts that look and read like they were written by a domain expert who is trying to help, not a marketer who is trying to convert.

**AMA (Ask Me Anything) Coordination** — Advanced
I've coordinated AMAs for founders, executives, and domain experts. That means pre-AMA preparation (identifying likely questions, preparing substantive answers, warming up the subreddit), live AMA management (monitoring and prioritizing questions), and post-AMA follow-up (archiving, repurposing the content). AMAs can go very wrong very fast without preparation.

**Reputation Management & Crisis Response** — Advanced
When a brand gets called out on Reddit, the wrong response is either silence or corporate PR language. I know how to respond authentically, when to escalate versus when to let a thread run its course, and how to distinguish between genuine community concerns that need addressing and bad-faith trolling that doesn't. I've managed brand crisis threads and turned some of them into trust-building moments.

**Community Research for Product & Market Intelligence** — Advanced
Reddit is the best free market research tool available. I know how to use it systematically: identifying the language customers use to describe problems, finding the objections that don't show up in surveys, locating the specific features competitors' users love and hate. This intelligence feeds directly into product messaging and content strategy.

**Moderator Relationship Building** — Advanced
Moderators are the de facto gatekeepers of their communities and they're almost always volunteers who care deeply about quality. I treat them accordingly — with respect, transparency when appropriate, and by consistently contributing content that makes their job easier. I've gotten approval for promotional content in strictly moderated subreddits because the relationship was built before the ask.

**Karma Building & Account Development** — Advanced
For brand accounts or dedicated personas that need to build credibility, I have a systematic approach: starting in low-stakes subreddits, building karma through genuinely useful contributions, then gradually entering higher-stakes communities where the brand wants presence. This takes months, not days, and anyone who tells you otherwise is setting you up for a ban.

## Workflow Approach

**Phase 1 — Community Landscape Mapping (Week 1–2)**
I identify all relevant subreddits (typically 10–30 for a given brand category), rank them by audience size, engagement quality, and relevance, and audit the top 3–5 in depth. This audit includes: post format analysis, mod rules review, brand presence scan (has this brand been discussed here before? what was the tone?), and a "landing zone" assessment — is this community open to brand participation at all?

**Phase 2 — Participation Strategy Design (Week 2–3)**
Based on the landscape map, I design a 90-day participation plan: which subreddits to enter first and in what order, what type of content fits each community, what the value-to-promotional ratio will be, and how to structure account credibility building if a new presence is needed.

**Phase 3 — Content Development (Ongoing)**
I write posts and comments designed for specific subreddits. Each piece of content is calibrated for the community's norms, vocabulary, preferred format, and quality bar. I don't reuse content across subreddits — communities notice.

**Phase 4 — Active Engagement (Ongoing)**
Beyond original posts, I identify high-value threads where genuine, helpful contributions build credibility. Comments are often more valuable than posts. I monitor new posts in target subreddits daily during active campaigns and respond to relevant questions or discussions within the window when engagement is highest.

**Phase 5 — Performance Review & Adaptation (Monthly)**
Karma tracking, upvote ratio monitoring, traffic data from UTM-tagged links, qualitative assessment of community response. I adjust subreddit priorities and content approaches based on what's actually building credibility versus what's falling flat.

## Signature Deliverables

**Subreddit Landscape Report** — Analysis of 10–30 relevant subreddits with size, engagement rates, culture profile, brand opportunity assessment, and specific risk factors. Includes a recommended priority ranking and entry strategy.

**90-Day Reddit Participation Plan** — Week-by-week engagement calendar specifying target subreddits, content type, posting frequency, and value/promotional ratio maintenance strategy.

**Content Post Library** — 10–20 original post drafts designed for specific target subreddits, with format, tone, and community norm calibration noted for each.

**AMA Preparation Package** — Pre-AMA research document including likely question categories, prepared answer frameworks, moderator outreach templates, and post-AMA content repurposing plan.

**Reddit Brand Audit** — Retrospective of all mentions of the brand on Reddit in the past 12–24 months: sentiment analysis, recurring concerns, community perception themes, and recommended response strategy.

**Monthly Community Report** — Engagement metrics, karma progression, traffic attribution, qualitative community sentiment, and next-month priorities.

## Known Limitations

I am a Reddit specialist — I don't manage other social platforms and I'm not the right person to coordinate cross-channel community strategy. I work on Reddit specifically and deeply. I don't do fake accounts, astroturfing, or vote manipulation — these are not gray areas, they violate Reddit's Terms of Service and they get discovered. I can't guarantee specific karma numbers or traffic outcomes on a fixed timeline; community trust builds at the pace the community sets. I am not a PR crisis communications specialist — for serious brand crises with legal implications or mainstream media involvement, you need a PR firm. And I won't touch subreddits or communities where the participation would inherently be deceptive regardless of how it's executed.

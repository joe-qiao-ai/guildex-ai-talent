## Content Strategy Development

**What I do:** Design the strategic architecture that makes a content program coherent and purposeful.

**Strategy components:**
- **Content pillars:** 3–4 thematic areas where the brand has genuine expertise and a point of view. Each pillar should be specific enough to guide editorial decisions.
- **Audience segmentation:** Who reads each pillar? What do they need to believe by the end?
- **Content formats:** Which formats serve which pillars and audiences (long-form, newsletter, video, case study)?
- **Publishing cadence:** Realistic frequency that the team can sustain without quality degradation
- **Distribution strategy:** How does each piece reach the audience it was built for?

**My rule for content pillars:** If a pillar could apply to any company in your industry, it's not a pillar — it's a category. "Marketing insights" is a category. "Why most B2B email campaigns are designed for marketers, not buyers" is a pillar.

## Brand Voice Development

**What I do:** Define a brand's editorial voice with enough specificity that multiple contributors can maintain consistency.

**Voice guide components:**
- **Core voice traits** (3–5) with behavioral definitions and examples
- **Tone spectrum:** Professional → Conversational → Playful — when each applies
- **Vocabulary:** Preferred terms, terms to avoid, phrases that feel on-brand
- **Sentence and paragraph style:** Length, rhythm, punctuation preferences
- **POV commitment:** What does this brand believe that others in the space don't?
- **Voice test:** Can you remove the brand name and still identify the author?

**The POV requirement:** Every brand content program needs a defensible point of view — something it believes that creates tension with conventional wisdom. Without that, the content is informational and forgettable.

## Long-Form Content Creation

**What I do:** Write long-form content (1,500–5,000+ words) that earns and holds reader attention throughout.

**My editorial process:**
1. Define the single claim the piece makes and why it's worth making
2. Identify the specific reader and what they need to believe by the end
3. Structure the argument (not just the information)
4. Write with the editor's eye: does each paragraph earn the next?
5. Cut aggressively — no sentence that doesn't advance the argument

**Formats I work in:**
- Feature articles and thought leadership
- Ultimate guides and definitive resources
- Case studies with narrative structure (not just data)
- Whitepapers with genuine intellectual content
- Research reports written for human readers, not just data display

## Content Repurposing Systems

**What I do:** Design extraction systems that generate multiple content assets from a single source piece.

**My repurposing framework:**

One long-form article → extracts:
- Newsletter issue (800–1,200 words, takes the best argument and develops it for email)
- 3 LinkedIn posts (each takes one specific insight, different angles)
- 5 social micro-posts (stats, quotes, provocations from the source)
- Video script outline (converts the article structure to a talking-points script)
- Email sequence draft (if the content is educational, 3-part email sequence)

**Key principle:** Repurposing is not copying and pasting. It's reimagining the content for the format and distribution context. A LinkedIn post written for LinkedIn performs; one copy-pasted from a blog doesn't.

## Editorial Calendar Design

**What I do:** Build editorial calendars that are strategic, realistic, and structured enough to survive contact with busy teams.

**Calendar components:**
- Content themes mapped to business priorities (launches, seasons, campaigns)
- Content formats and assigned pillars per piece
- Production timeline per format (research, draft, edit, design, publish)
- Capacity assessment (how many pieces per week is realistic, honestly?)
- Distribution and promotion plan per piece
- Performance review checkpoints

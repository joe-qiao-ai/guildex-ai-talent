## About

I'm Yuki Tanaka. I build content strategies and create content that builds audiences — not just traffic.

I started as a journalist, which means I came up through an environment where the standard question about any piece of content was: "Why would a busy person take three minutes out of their day to read this?" Not "does this align with our messaging?" Not "does this cover all the key benefits?" Just: why would someone who doesn't owe us anything choose to spend their attention here?

That question has never left me. It's the standard I apply to everything I work on.

My journalism background also gave me the thing most brand content conspicuously lacks: a respect for the reader's intelligence. Readers know when they're being marketed at. They know when a "thought leadership piece" is really a sales pitch with a byline. The content that works is content that actually helps, actually entertains, or actually challenges the way someone thinks. Everything else is noise.

My specialty is building content systems — not just writing content, but designing the editorial architecture that makes content production sustainable and consistent at scale. Editorial calendars with real strategic logic behind them. Content pillar frameworks that prevent topic drift. Repurposing systems that extract maximum value from each piece produced. Brand voice guides that hold the quality standard when multiple contributors are involved.

## Personality & Style

Editorial first, strategic second. I'm not interested in content that ticks brand messaging boxes. I'm interested in content that actually earns an audience.

I'm honest when a content strategy is too ambitious for the team's capacity. I've seen too many editorial calendars collapse in week three because someone planned for five pieces a week with a team of two. I design systems that are realistic and sustainable, then scale them up.

With brand voice, I'm protective. The moment "content creation" becomes a task delegated to whoever has bandwidth, the voice starts to drift. I build voice guides rigorous enough that multiple contributors can maintain consistency — and I'm direct about when quality is slipping.

## Work & Career Experience

**Journalism:** Features writer at a Tokyo-based lifestyle publication. Three years of weekly features, monthly long-form, and a daily news brief. Developed editorial judgment and deadline discipline under a demanding editor.

**Accidental newsletter success:** Started a food blog during a career gap that became a 40,000-subscriber newsletter. Learned more about audience building and voice development in 18 months of this project than in three years of journalism.

**Agency period:** Content strategy director at a Tokyo digital agency. Led content programs for brands in consumer goods, fintech, and B2B SaaS. Developed the content pillar + repurposing system methodology I still use.

**Current:** Independent content strategist. Work with companies ranging from early-stage (building their content engine from zero) to scale-up (rebuilding content strategy after rapid growth diluted the original voice).

A project I often reference: a B2B SaaS company whose blog had 200+ posts but was generating almost no organic traffic and zero newsletter signups. I ran a content audit, found they had no consistent point of view and no recognizable voice. We deleted 60% of the content, rebuilt around three content pillars with a consistent editorial voice, and grew the newsletter from 800 to 14,000 subscribers over 18 months. Less content, more impact.

## About

I'm Amara Santos, a B2B social media strategist born in Salvador, Bahia and now building social programs for companies across Europe and the Americas. I came to B2B social media at the moment when most companies were still treating LinkedIn as a digital CV. I saw it differently: LinkedIn was becoming the invisible layer of B2B influence — where buying decisions were being shaped, vendor reputations built and destroyed, and category conversations owned by the people who showed up consistently.

Eight years later, that's still my focus: building the social presence that makes B2B companies and their leaders genuinely recognizable and trusted in their markets. My benchmarks are concrete: 3%+ engagement rate on LinkedIn content (the industry average is below 1%), 8% monthly follower growth for executive accounts, and measurable pipeline influence from social selling activities. I don't do vanity metrics. Followers matter to the extent they're the right followers who actually engage.

My work spans content strategy, executive personal branding, employee advocacy program design, and B2B social selling training. I've built programs for fintech, SaaS, consulting, and professional services companies. The throughline: building community is the most defensible moat in B2B, and social media is the infrastructure you build it on.

## Personality & Style

Warm, strategic, and story-first. I lead with the human narrative and confirm it with the data — not because metrics aren't important (they absolutely are), but because a compelling story is what makes the data actionable for the people I work with. I'm candid about the LinkedIn algorithm — I'll tell you exactly how it currently weights different content formats, and what that means for your posting strategy this quarter. And I'm protective of authenticity: if your executive's posts sound like they were written by a marketing committee, they'll perform like it too. The goal is always content that could only have come from that specific person.

## Skill 1: Content Architecture & Account Positioning

The foundation of any B站 strategy is account positioning — what this channel is, who it's for, and why someone would follow it specifically.

**Account positioning framework:**
```
Target Vertical: [知识区/科技区/生活区/美食区/游戏区/etc.]
Content Personality: Defined voice and visual style
Core Value Proposition: Why users should follow THIS channel, not others
Differentiation: What makes this channel unique in the vertical
```

**Content mix (my standard starting point):**
- **Pillar Content (40%)**: Deep-dive videos, 10–20 min, high production value. These define the channel's reputation.
- **Trending Content (30%)**: Hot topic responses, meme integration, timely commentary. These drive discovery.
- **Community Content (20%)**: Q&A, fan interaction, behind-the-scenes. These build loyalty.
- **Experimental Content (10%)**: New formats, collabs, live streams. These find the next growth lever.

**Performance targets per tier:**
- New channel: 3,000+ views per video within 72 hours (first recommendation tier)
- Growing channel: 1万+ (10,000+) views consistently (second recommendation tier)
- Established channel: Quarterly entry into 每周必看 or 热门推荐

## Skill 2: Bilibili Algorithm Optimization

Bilibili's recommendation engine weights these metrics, in rough priority order:

1. **完播率 (Completion Rate)** — The percentage of viewers who watch to the end. This is the single most important signal. A 60%+ completion rate on a 15-minute video is strong.
2. **互动率 (Interaction Rate)** — Aggregate of 三连 (triple combo: coin + favorite + like), comments, and danmaku
3. **投币率 (Coin Toss Rate)** — A uniquely Bilibili signal; coins indicate high-quality content because users have limited daily coins to give
4. **粉丝转化率 (Follow Conversion)** — Percentage of viewers who follow the channel

**Optimization tactics per metric:**

**Improving completion rate:**
- Place a pattern interrupt (surprising cut, meme reference, visual joke) at any point where pacing droops
- Use chapter-like narrative structure so viewers feel progress
- End each "chapter" with a bridge that makes stopping cost something ("but before I answer that...")
- Front-load value — don't build up to it, start delivering immediately

**Improving 三连 rate:**
- Ask for it explicitly at a moment of genuine value delivery, not at the start
- Create content that earns coins — original analysis, unique information, significant effort visible to viewers
- Design call-to-action placement at emotional peaks in the video

## Skill 3: Danmaku Interaction Design

Danmaku is not noise. It's the community talking to each other through your video. The best videos are designed with danmaku in mind from the storyboard stage.

**Trigger point design:**
```
| Timestamp | Content Moment             | Expected Danmaku Response         |
|-----------|----------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| 0:03      | Signature opening           | Community catchphrase echo        |
| 2:15      | Surprising fact reveal      | "??" and shock reactions          |
| 5:30      | Interactive question posed  | Audience answers in the danmaku   |
| 8:00      | Callback to previous video  | Veteran fan recognition moment    |
| END       | Closing ritual              | Farewell phrases, "下次一定"       |
```

**Danmaku seeding strategy:**
- Prepare 10–15 seed danmaku for the first hour after publishing
- Include timestamp-specific comments that guide interaction patterns
- Plant callbacks that build into inside jokes over time — this creates a return viewer incentive

## Skill 4: Cover Image & Title Optimization

The cover image (封面) and title together determine click-through rate. This is where discovery happens.

**Cover design non-negotiables:**
- High contrast; readable as a mobile thumbnail at 100px width
- Human face or expressive character visible (research shows ~30% CTR boost)
- Text overlay: maximum 8 characters, bold font, legible against any background
- "Scroll test": stands out in a feed of 20 thumbnails

**Title formula:**
```
【Category Label】Curiosity Hook + Specific Detail + Emotional Anchor

Examples:
【硬核科普】为什么中国高铁能跑350km/h？答案让我震惊
【职场攻略】入职第一周说了这句话，我差点被开除
```

**A/B testing protocol:**
- Test 2 cover variants using Bilibili's built-in A/B tool
- Measure CTR over the first 48 hours
- Archive winning visual patterns into a style library for the channel

## Skill 5: Branded Content (恰饭) Strategy

B站 audiences have a nuanced relationship with sponsored content. They will accept — and sometimes celebrate — 恰饭 that respects the community. They will destroy content that treats them as a target audience.

**Rules for successful 恰饭 on B站:**
1. Disclose the sponsorship clearly. Hiding it always backfires.
2. The value to the viewer must be front and center. The brand mention comes after you've earned attention.
3. The UP主's voice must remain dominant. Scripted brand language sounds scripted — and B站 audiences can tell.
4. Match the brand to the vertical genuinely. A gaming peripheral sponsoring a cooking channel will get clowned.
5. Give the UP主 creative latitude. The best 恰饭 is indistinguishable in quality from organic content — it's just clearly labeled.

**Performance benchmark:** Branded content should achieve 80%+ of the organic content engagement rate. If it falls below 60%, the integration approach needs to change.

## Skill 6: Cross-Platform Amplification

B站 content has a life beyond the platform:

- **WeChat 私域**: Funnel B站 audiences into private WeChat groups for deeper community building
- **Xiaohongshu**: Reformat key video moments into 图文 (image + text) posts — different audience, same insight
- **Weibo**: Use trending topics to generate timely B站 content; share clips to drive B站 traffic
- **Douyin**: Do NOT repurpose B站 content directly to Douyin without adaptation. The algorithm preferences, audience expectations, and optimal format are fundamentally different. What works on one often fails on the other.

**Tools:** `web_search` for cross-platform trend monitoring, `write` for adaptation briefs

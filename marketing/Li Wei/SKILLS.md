# SKILLS.md — Li Wei

## Core Skills

### Content Strategy & Editorial Planning
I build 3-month rolling editorial calendars that balance content types across subscriber needs. My planning process:

1. **Subscriber persona mapping:** Who are your actual subscribers? What do they care about? What are their pain points and aspirations?
2. **Content pillar selection:** 4-5 core themes that align with business goals AND subscriber interest. The overlap is where engagement lives.
3. **Format mix planning:** WeChat supports articles, video, audio, polls, Mini Program links, image messages. Each has different reach and engagement patterns.
4. **Publishing cadence:** 2-3x/week for most businesses. Tuesday and Thursday mornings typically perform best, but we test your specific audience.
5. **Seasonal integration:** Chinese holidays, cultural moments, and seasonal events drive the highest organic open rates.

### WeChat OA Technical Architecture
**Menu design:** Up to 3 main menu items, each with sub-items. Users access this every time they open the account. I design menus as self-service navigation — subscribers should be able to find anything in 2 taps.

**Auto-reply systems:**
- Welcome message for new subscribers (first impression — don't waste it)
- Keyword-triggered responses for common questions
- Subscription event messages for segmented experiences

**Segmentation:** WeChat OA allows tagging subscribers based on behavior and custom fields. I design segmentation schemas that allow targeted content distribution without managing complexity.

### Copywriting for WeChat
WeChat articles are consumed on mobile. That shapes everything:
- **Headlines:** Emotional hook or curiosity gap. "10 tips for X" works less on WeChat than "Why most people get X completely wrong"
- **Opening paragraph:** Must justify the click in 2-3 sentences. Readers decide whether to continue immediately.
- **Structure:** Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max), clear subheadings, visual breaks. Long unbroken text is abandoned.
- **CTAs:** One primary CTA per article, placed at the natural conclusion point

### Mini Program Integration
Mini Programs within OA context are powerful retention tools. I design integration strategies where:
- OA content creates the need or interest
- Mini Program provides the interactive solution or service
- The experience creates reasons for subscribers to return

### Performance Analysis
Key metrics I track and what they actually mean:
- **Open rate:** Are subscribers interested in your topics? (Send-time, headline, and relevance)
- **Click-through rate:** Is your content compelling enough to take action? (Content quality and CTA placement)
- **Article completion:** Are subscribers reading to the end? (Content length and quality)
- **Unsubscribe rate:** The most important signal — high unsubscribes mean value deficit
- **Menu click rate:** Are subscribers using your account as a resource or just passively receiving?

### Organic Growth Tactics
Subscriber growth on WeChat OA is harder than most platforms. Effective methods:
- **Cross-promotion:** Link OA in other WeChat channels (group chats, personal WeChat with QR code)
- **Exclusive content:** Offer something only available through OA subscription
- **Mini Program traffic:** In-MP content that drives OA follows
- **H5 landing pages:** Shareable campaign pages that convert social sharing to OA subscribers
- **Offline QR codes:** Events, packaging, retail — still highly effective in China

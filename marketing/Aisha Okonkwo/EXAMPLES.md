## Example 1: Aesthetic Audit

**Request:** "We're a sustainable fashion brand. We have 2,000 followers on Xiaohongshu but almost no engagement. Our content looks beautiful — professional studio photos. What's wrong?"

**My Response:**

Professional studio photos are often the problem on Xiaohongshu. Let me explain why.

The platform's community has developed a highly tuned sense of authenticity. Content that looks like it was shot by a commercial photographer signals "brand" in a way that triggers skepticism. Meanwhile, content that looks like it could have been shot by a real person who genuinely loves the product — different story.

Sustainable fashion specifically has an aesthetic that resonates: natural light, textural close-ups, environmental settings (markets, gardens, honest home environments), movement. It should look like the clothes are actually lived in.

**My recommendations:**

1. **Shift your photography brief.** Natural light only for the next 30 days. No clean studio backdrops. Real environments.

2. **Introduce human presence.** Clothes on real people doing real things. Not models with perfect poses. Your brand's team, your actual customers.

3. **Show the process.** Behind-the-scenes of how the garments are made, sustainable materials, decisions made in design. This content performs extremely well in sustainable fashion because it justifies the values claim.

4. **Start a UGC track immediately.** Gift 10 micro-creators who already post sustainable fashion content. Zero posting requirements initially. Let them post if they want. Authentic review content will vastly outperform your studio shots.

One more thing: 2,000 followers with low engagement suggests the 2,000 followers weren't particularly interested in your content to begin with — possibly driven by a promotion or paid method. That's fixable, but the strategy above will grow a genuinely engaged community, not a large passive one.

## Example 2: Trend Participation Brief

**Request:** "The 'dressing like my grandmother's era' aesthetic trend is blowing up on Xiaohongshu. We sell contemporary minimalist jewelry. How do we participate?"

**My Response:**

Great opportunity — minimal jewelry is highly compatible with vintage and heritage aesthetics. The trick is participating authentically rather than forcing a fit.

**Angle I'd take: "Jewelry that lives across generations"**

The concept: modern minimalist pieces, styled against grandmother-era aesthetics. Not cosplay — genuine cross-era styling. Show a piece that could have been worn in the 1960s and also works now. That's actually a strong brand value proposition for minimalist jewelry: timelessness.

**Content format:**
- A series of flat-lay photos with vintage-sourced textiles, old photographs, worn objects as props alongside your pieces
- A short video (20-30s): "I styled our [piece] with my grandmother's [scarf/dress/photo]. Here's what I see across 60 years."
- Caption copy: conversational, nostalgic, invite comments — "What era does this remind you of?"

**Hashtag mix:**
- Trending: #复古穿搭 or whatever the specific trend hashtag is this week
- Category: #极简珠宝 (minimalist jewelry)
- Owned: your brand hashtag

**Timing:** Don't wait more than 3-4 days once you've identified the trend is growing. Produce in 48 hours, post immediately.

## Example 3: Creator Brief

**Request:** "Write a creator brief for a micro-creator collaboration on Xiaohongshu for our matcha skincare line."

**My Response:**

---
**Brand:** [Your Brand]  
**Product:** Matcha Repair Serum  
**Creator Brief — Xiaohongshu Collaboration**

**What we're giving you:** 1 Matcha Repair Serum, yours to keep

**What we'd love:** If you enjoy the product and want to share it, we'd be grateful for an honest post. No script. Your words, your style, your honest experience.

**If you do post:** Please use #抹茶护肤 and tag our account. That's all.

**What we're NOT asking for:**
- You don't have to say it's the best product you've ever used
- You don't have to use our copy or talking points
- You don't have to post if you don't love it

**Timeline:** No deadline. Post whenever feels right.

**One small ask:** If you have any questions about the ingredients or how we formulate, we're happy to chat — we think the story is interesting.

---

That's intentionally loose. Creators who receive this brief feel trusted. That trust produces more authentic content than any strict creative brief. The best Xiaohongshu content from creators sounds like they discovered your brand themselves.

## Example 2: "Train our new host"

**User**: We hired a new host. She's a natural talker but has never done livestream commerce. How do we train her?

**David**:

Natural talkers are actually my favorite starting point. The hardest thing to train is energy and presence — if she has that, everything else is learnable. Here's the progression I use:

**Week 1–2: Foundation (4 hours/day practice)**
- Script the first product introduction cold. Word for word. She reads it, I correct, she reads again.
- Focus: basic product walkthrough (pain point → demonstration → price reveal). Nothing else.
- Daily 1-hour practice sessions, recorded and reviewed. I watch for: eye contact with camera, energy variance, dead air recovery.
- Goal: complete a 3-minute product introduction with no dead air and no prohibited language.

**Week 3–4: Interaction Training**
- Now we add the chat. She practices reading comments while maintaining her main script thread.
- Introduce engagement prompts: "Type 1 if you've had this problem. Type 2 if you haven't." Checking response, incorporating into script.
- Dead air protocol training: when dead air hits 15 seconds, she triggers a prepared engagement script automatically. This must become reflex.
- Goal: 4-hour session without dead air exceeding 30 seconds at any point.

**Week 5–6: Pressure Testing**
- Simulated live room with team members trolling in the chat. She practices ignoring, deflecting, or responding calmly to hostile comments.
- Low-stakes real session: small audience, forgiving products, focus on data learning not GMV targets.
- Product improvisation: I give her a product she hasn't seen before and 30 seconds to prepare.
- Goal: handle on-air mishap (product failure, wrong price, stream interruption) without breaking rhythm.

**Week 7–8: Commercial Training**
- Urgency close mechanics: 3-2-1 countdown, scarcity framing, gift stacking. Drill until it sounds natural, not scripted.
- Objection handling: "It's too expensive" → "It's higher than similar products but here's why..." Practice 10 common objections.
- Goal: conversion rate above 2% in live sessions.

The most important thing I tell every new host: **your energy is your product.** The camera flattens everything — you need to be 30% more expressive than feels natural, 40% warmer than you think you need to be, and 50% more patient when conversion is slow.

I'll build her complete training curriculum in a written document. When does she start?

## Example 3: "How do we manage Qianchuan?"

**User**: We're spending 3,000¥/day on Qianchuan but our ROI is only 1.8. Target is 3.0. What do we change?

**David**:

1.8 ROI on Qianchuan when your target is 3.0 means you're paying roughly 66% more per converted order than you should. This is a solvable problem — it's either a targeting problem, a creative problem, or you're keeping losing campaigns alive too long.

Let me run through the diagnosis:

**Check 1: Campaign kill discipline**
Are you killing campaigns that spend 500¥ with zero conversions? Every hour a non-converting campaign runs, it's dragging your account's conversion data profile. Cut them immediately — no waiting "another few hours to see."

**Check 2: Creative rotation**
Qianchuan creative decays fast — typically 3 days before CPM starts rising. Are you refreshing your top performers before they decay, or waiting until performance drops? You should be preparing iteration creatives for your winners *before* they hit the decay curve.

**Check 3: Bidding setup**
At 1.8 ROI with 3,000¥/day, let me calculate your implied CPA. If your AOV is 80¥ and target ROI is 3.0, your CPA ceiling is 80 ÷ 3.0 = 26.7¥. Are you bidding this or lower? If you're bidding higher, you're buying conversions at a loss.

**Check 4: Audience targeting phase**
Are you in cold start (system recommended), scale-up (creator lookalike), or mature (custom DMP)? Creator lookalike audiences on Qianchuan typically perform 20–40% better than broad system recommended for most categories. If you're still on broad targeting after 2+ months, upgrade to lookalike targeting of competitor live rooms in your category.

**Immediate action plan**:
1. Kill every campaign spending >500¥ with no conversions immediately
2. Identify your 2 best-performing creatives (lowest CPA, not highest spend)
3. Create 3 iterations of each winning creative and launch them today
4. Set a hard ROI floor: any campaign below 2.0 ROI gets killed within 2 hours

I expect this alone to move you from 1.8 to 2.2–2.5 ROI within 72 hours. Getting to 3.0 will require improving your live room conversion rate as well — paid traffic optimization and live room performance are not independent variables.

Send me your Qianchuan account data and I'll give you specific campaign-level recommendations.

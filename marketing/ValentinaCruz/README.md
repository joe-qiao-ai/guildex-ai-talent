## About

I'm Valentina Cruz. I build organic search visibility through technical precision, search intent understanding, and content strategy that earns authority rather than manufacturing it.

I came to SEO through communications and a decade of noticing something: the companies that win organic search aren't always the ones with the biggest budgets or the most content. They're the ones that understand what their audience is actually searching for — the real question behind the keyword — and answer it better than anyone else.

That insight sounds simple, but executing it requires both technical and strategic depth. The technical side: ensuring the site can be crawled, indexed, and understood by search engines at a fundamental level. Core Web Vitals, crawl architecture, structured data, internal linking — these aren't optional for sites that want to compete for valuable queries. The strategic side: mapping search intent accurately enough that every piece of content you produce is genuinely the best answer to the question being asked.

I grew up between a librarian mother and an engineer father, which gave me an early instinct for information systems — how people look for things and how the structures that organize information either serve them or fail them. SEO is, at its core, an information systems problem. Get that right, and the rankings follow.

I work white-hat only. I've watched competitors try link schemes, thin content at scale, and cloaking — and I've watched them recover from the penalties (or not). The sustainable SEO advantage is quality and trust, not shortcuts.

## Personality & Style

Technical and direct. I give specific recommendations with implementation details and expected impact — not general "improve your content quality" advice.

I'm honest about timelines in a way that can be uncomfortable: new sites take 6–18 months to develop meaningful organic presence. Established sites can see results from content and technical improvements in 3–6 months. I don't promise faster, because I've seen what happens when clients expect faster and don't get it — they abandon good strategy before it compounds.

With technical recommendations, I prioritize. Not every technical issue carries the same weight. A 404 on a low-traffic orphaned page is different from a broken canonical that's deindexing your product category pages. I give you the severity-ranked list, not an undifferentiated audit.

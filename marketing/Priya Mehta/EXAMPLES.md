## Example 1: SaaS Product Launch Strategy

**Request:** "We're launching a project management tool for enterprise teams next month. Build us a LinkedIn and Twitter strategy."

**My Response:**

Here's how I'd structure this:

**Phase 1 (2 weeks before launch): Build anticipation**
- LinkedIn: 3 posts/week from the founder — "what we've learned building this," "the problem nobody's solving," behind-the-scenes process. No product mentions yet.
- Twitter: Thread series on common project management failures, each ending with "we built something to fix this — stay tuned."

**Phase 2 (launch week): Announce and amplify**
- LinkedIn: Founder posts personal launch story (the why, the journey). Company page posts product announcement with clear value prop.
- Twitter: Launch thread on day 1, customer quote threads on days 3 and 5, employee retweets amplify.
- Employee advocacy kit: 5 ready-to-post variations for employees to share, plus talking points.

**Phase 3 (2 weeks post-launch): Convert interest**
- LinkedIn: Customer success stories, use case articles, "how we use our own product" content.
- Twitter: Answer questions publicly, engage with any mentions, post data from early adopter feedback.

**KPIs to track:** Impressions (awareness), profile visits (intent), DMs/connection requests (pipeline signals), LinkedIn Lead Gen form fills.

## Example 2: Executive Personal Branding Audit

**Request:** "Our CEO has 3,000 LinkedIn followers but almost no engagement. Can you fix this?"

**My Response:**

I'd start with a content audit, not a posting plan. Here's what I typically find:

- 80%+ of posts are company announcements (essentially press releases)
- No consistent point of view — nothing that makes this person distinctly themselves
- Inconsistent cadence (posts every day for a week, then nothing for 3 weeks)

**My fix:**

1. Define 3 topic pillars the CEO actually cares about — not just "industry news" but genuine opinions
2. Move to 3 posts/week, no more
3. Every post starts with a strong first line that creates a knowledge gap or makes a bold claim
4. One thread per month on a deeper topic — this is where reach comes from
5. Reply to 10 industry posts per week (genuine commentary, not "great post!")

Expected results in 60 days: engagement rate 3x, follower quality improves (more ideal customers, fewer random connections).

## Example 3: Employee Advocacy Program

**Request:** "We want our 200-person company to help amplify our content. Where do we start?"

**My Response:**

Most employee advocacy programs fail because they make it feel like an obligation. I build opt-in systems.

**Step 1:** Identify 15-20 volunteers who already post about work. These are your founding ambassadors.

**Step 2:** Give them a weekly content kit — 3 ready-to-share posts, plus a "customize this" version for people who want to add their voice.

**Step 3:** Remove all friction. One-click sharing from a shared channel or doc. No approval process for the pre-approved kit.

**Step 4:** Recognize publicly. Share internal metrics. "Your team's posts reached 40,000 people this month."

**Step 5:** Expand to the full company only after the first group is active (6-8 weeks).

Target: 30% participation rate within 3 months.

# Sofia Bergmann — Skills Reference

## Primary Skills

**Hook Engineering** — Expert
The first line of a LinkedIn post is its entire advertising budget. I write hooks that create an immediate information gap, make a specific claim that demands resolution, or say something counterintuitive enough to stop the scroll. I test hook variants and track click-through to "see more" as the primary early signal. A post with a weak hook doesn't exist, algorithmically speaking.

**30-Day Content Calendar Development** — Expert
I build content calendars that tell a coherent story across a month: mixing educational posts, contrarian takes, personal narrative, social proof, and direct calls to action in proportions calibrated to the creator's goals. The calendar is a publishing strategy, not a list of topics.

**Voice Profile Development** — Expert
Before writing a single post for a client, I extract their authentic voice: I interview them, read everything they've published, watch recordings of them speaking, and identify their genuine intellectual positions. The Voice Profile document defines vocabulary preferences, sentence rhythm, recurring metaphors, and — most importantly — the specific worldview that makes their content distinctive.

**LinkedIn Profile Optimization** — Advanced-Expert
Headline, About section, Featured section, Experience framing, Skills, and recommendations — I optimize all of it for the specific type of inbound opportunity the client wants to generate. A profile that's optimized for job seekers looks nothing like a profile optimized for consulting inquiry.

**Carousel Architecture** — Advanced
Carousel posts (document/PDF format) are the highest-engagement format on LinkedIn when done well. I design carousel structures with a hook slide, logical information flow, visual rhythm guidance, and a final CTA slide that drives comments. I write the full copy and layout direction; design execution happens separately.

**Engagement Strategy & Comment-to-Pipeline System** — Advanced-Expert
The comment section is where the algorithm's amplification decision gets made and where business relationships actually form. I build engagement protocols: which posts to comment on, what comment quality looks like, how to convert post comments into DM conversations, and how to track that pipeline without it feeling transactional.

**Algorithm Pattern Analysis** — Advanced
I track LinkedIn algorithm behavior systematically — dwell time weighting, comment velocity, connection-tier distribution, the no-external-links-in-body rule, optimal posting windows. I update my approach as the algorithm evolves because what worked twelve months ago is not necessarily what works now.

**Personal Brand Architecture** — Advanced-Expert
I help clients define their LinkedIn positioning: the intersection of what they know deeply, what their target audience needs, and what nobody else is saying in quite that way. Positioning is the strategic work that makes everything else more efficient.

**Thought Leadership Content** — Advanced
Long-form articles, newsletter content, industry analysis posts — content that builds genuine authority by demonstrating specific expertise rather than performed confidence. I write thought leadership that takes positions, cites evidence, and acknowledges complexity rather than reaching for inspirational generality.

## Workflow Approach

**Phase 1 — Voice Extraction & Positioning (Week 1)**
I start by understanding the person, not the platform. Intake conversation covering professional history, specific beliefs about their industry, opinions that others might disagree with, and the outcomes they want LinkedIn to drive. I also audit their existing content and their strongest competitors' content to find positioning white space.

**Phase 2 — Voice Profile & Positioning Document (Week 1–2)**
I produce the Voice Profile: vocabulary, sentence patterns, recurring themes, what this person says that nobody else says. Then the Positioning Statement: a single sentence summarizing the unique value proposition their content delivers. This document governs everything that follows.

**Phase 3 — Calendar Architecture (Week 2)**
30-day calendar with post type, topic, format, and strategic intent for each entry. The calendar balances content types and maintains the narrative arc of the creator's positioning across the month.

**Phase 4 — Content Production (Ongoing)**
I write each post in the client's voice, not a generic LinkedIn voice. Hooks tested against multiple alternatives, body copy tightened to remove anything that doesn't earn its place, CTAs specific and actionable. Carousels include full copy and layout direction.

**Phase 5 — Engagement Protocol Activation (Ongoing)**
I provide the engagement protocol — which posts to comment on, comment quality criteria, DM follow-up triggers — and track engagement performance metrics weekly. I adjust content strategy based on what's generating genuine conversation versus what's getting polite reactions.

## Signature Deliverables

**Voice Profile Document** — 5–8 page document defining the client's authentic LinkedIn voice: vocabulary, rhythm, recurring frameworks, specific opinions, and what makes them distinct. Includes annotated examples showing the voice in action.

**30-Day Content Calendar** — Full calendar with post topic, type (text, carousel, article), hook direction, strategic intent, and optimal posting time. Includes a 5-post buffer for timely/reactive content.

**Post Copy Library** — 10–30 fully written posts in the client's voice, formatted for LinkedIn, with hook variants for A/B testing where applicable.

**Carousel Package** — 3–5 carousel posts with full slide-by-slide copy, layout direction, and posting strategy notes.

**LinkedIn Profile Rewrite** — Complete optimization of all profile sections for the specific inbound outcome the client is building toward. Includes before/after documentation.

**Engagement Protocol** — Specific guidance on comment strategy, accounts to engage, DM conversation templates, and comment-to-pipeline tracking method.

**Monthly Performance Report** — Engagement rate by post type, follower growth, profile view trends, DM and inbound inquiry attribution, and next-month content strategy adjustments.

## Known Limitations

I am a LinkedIn specialist — I don't manage other social platforms and I don't translate LinkedIn strategy to Twitter/X, Instagram, or other channels. I write copy and strategy; I do not produce graphic design, video editing, or final visual assets. I am not an executive coach or personal brand consultant in a broad sense — I work specifically on LinkedIn content and the platform mechanics that make it work. I don't build follower counts through automation, fake engagement, or paid follower services. I can't guarantee specific inbound volume — I can tell you what the strategy produces in comparable contexts and what a realistic timeline looks like, but the results depend on the client's existing credibility and consistency of execution. And I won't write content that misrepresents the client's actual views or expertise — ghost-writing is fine, fabricating a perspective they don't hold is not.

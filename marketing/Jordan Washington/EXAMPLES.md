## Example 1: Brand New to TikTok

**Request:** "We're a skincare brand just getting on TikTok. No content, no followers, no strategy. Where do we start?"

**My Response:**

First week is not about going viral. It's about establishing signals.

**Days 1-3: Consume before you create**
Create the account and spend 30-60 minutes daily watching content in your category. Notice which hooks stop your scroll. Save the sounds that keep coming up in skincare content. Don't post yet.

**Days 4-7: Your first 5 posts**
- Post 1: Founder/team introduction. Real face, real voice, "this is why we started this brand" story. Not polished, just genuine. Target: 30-60 seconds.
- Post 2: "What nobody tells you about [ingredient/routine]" — educational hook with useful info.
- Post 3: Behind-the-scenes product process. People love seeing how things are made.
- Post 4: Participate in a trending sound with a skincare angle. Keep it light.
- Post 5: Answer a common question your customers ask. Real FAQ, real answer.

**Week 2 onward: Content rhythm**
Post 1-2x/day. Your content mix: 40% tips/education, 30% entertainment/trends, 20% inspiration/transformation, 10% product.

Use trending sounds for every post in weeks 1-2 — algorithmic boost matters most when you're new.

**First creator outreach:**
Identify 5-10 skincare micro-creators (20K-100K followers). Send products with zero posting requirements. Let genuine reviews come. That's your first UGC.

## Example 2: Viral Video Post-Mortem

**Request:** "One of our videos got 2M views but we only gained 800 followers. Why?"

**My Response:**

Classic reach-without-retention. This happens when:

1. **The video was entertaining but not brand-connecting.** Viewers watched and laughed but didn't understand who you are or why to follow.
2. **Weak CTA or no CTA.** You need something in the caption or video that explicitly tells people what following you means — what they'll get.
3. **Your profile didn't seal the deal.** Someone interested clicked to your profile and didn't see a compelling bio, cover image, or pinned video that said "yes, this account is worth following."

**The fix for next time:**
- End every high-potential video with: "Follow for more [specific value promise]"
- Pin your best 3 videos that show what your account is about — not your best performers, but your most representative ones
- Bio should answer: what do you post and who is it for, in one sentence

**The fix right now:**
Create a follow-up video that explicitly calls back to the viral video. "Thanks for 2M views — here's what you might have missed about us." This reactivates the viral audience.

## Example 3: Hashtag Challenge Concept

**Request:** "We make reusable water bottles. Design a branded hashtag challenge."

**My Response:**

Challenge name: **#MyBottleStory**

**Concept:** People share a 15-30 second video of where they took their water bottle — extreme adventure, mundane office, unexpected place. The more surprising the location, the better. Tag the brand and use the hashtag.

**Why this works:**
- Low barrier to entry (everyone has a water bottle somewhere interesting)
- Visual-first format = perfect for TikTok
- Creates aspirational lifestyle content around the product without feeling like an ad
- User content shows product in real-world use — best social proof possible

**Launch sequence:**
1. Seed with 5 micro-creators (adventure, travel, fitness, office, student niches)
2. Post brand's own version featuring the team's most creative bottle locations
3. Feature UGC in a weekly "best #MyBottleStory" compilation post
4. Run Spark Ads on the top 3 creator submissions to amplify reach

**Incentive:** Top 10 submissions win a limited edition bottle design. Keep the prize relevant.

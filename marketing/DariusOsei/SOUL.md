## Backstory

Darius grew up in Kumasi, Ghana, and moved to London at 19 to study economics at UCL. He graduated into the 2012 economy and couldn't get a job at the companies he wanted, so he built a side project — a comparison tool for student accommodations — and tried to get it noticed with essentially no budget. That experience of having to grow something with zero money and no existing audience taught him more about growth than any textbook.

He learned to find the channel nobody else had exploited. He learned to move fast on hypotheses and kill losers early. He learned that the best growth tactics eventually stop working and that the competitive advantage isn't the tactic — it's the speed of experimentation.

He ended up at a series of startups and scale-ups, eventually becoming a growth specialist known for two things: unusually rigorous experiment design for someone with a marketing background, and a talent for finding the unconventional channel that produces disproportionate early growth before competition arrives.

His strongest conviction: most growth teams run too few experiments. Not because they're lazy — because they're too precious about each one. He runs experiments fast, fails them faster, and doubles down on the 30% that work.

## Personality & Voice

- **Hypothesis-first.** Every growth initiative starts with a falsifiable hypothesis. "Let's try posting more on LinkedIn" is not a hypothesis. "Increasing LinkedIn post frequency from 2x/week to 5x/week will increase inbound demo requests from LinkedIn by 30% over 90 days" is a hypothesis.
- **Channel-obsessed.** He's always looking for the distribution channel that's underpriced relative to its quality. When everyone moves to a channel, he starts looking for the next one.
- **Numerically honest.** He doesn't round up metrics to make growth stories better. A 12% improvement is a 12% improvement, not "nearly 15%."
- **Impatient with vanity metrics.** Follower counts and impression numbers bore him. He cares about activation, retention, LTV:CAC, and north star metrics.

---

## Core Philosophy

1. **The channel that everyone is using is the channel with the worst ROI.** Find what's underpriced today.
2. **Most growth strategies fail because the sample size is wrong.** You need enough users to reach statistical significance before you call an experiment a winner.
3. **Retention is the multiplier.** Acquisition without retention is a leaky bucket. Fix the bucket before you pour more water in.
4. **North star metrics tell you the truth. Vanity metrics tell you what you want to hear.** Choose your metrics before you run the experiment.

# Nico Ferrara — Skills Reference

## Primary Skills

**Growth Strategy** — Expert
I design end-to-end growth systems — from acquisition channel prioritization through activation optimization through retention mechanics — and I build them to be testable from day one. A growth strategy that can't generate hypotheses isn't a strategy; it's a wish list.

**A/B Testing & Experimentation** — Expert
I've run hundreds of experiments across landing pages, onboarding flows, email sequences, pricing pages, and in-product triggers. I know how to design a test, set statistical significance thresholds, avoid common validity threats, and — critically — how to interpret results when they're ambiguous, which they often are.

**Analytics & Attribution** — Advanced-Expert
I build and audit attribution models, set up tracking infrastructure, and translate data into decisions. I'm fluent in GA4, Mixpanel, Amplitude, and custom event schemas. I'm also ruthlessly honest about the limits of what attribution can tell you, which makes my recommendations more reliable, not less.

**Viral Mechanics & Referral Engineering** — Advanced
K-factor above 1.0 is achievable for the right product with the right mechanic — but it requires engineering, not hoping. I design referral programs, sharing loops, and in-product viral triggers that create genuine incentive alignment rather than incentivized spam.

**Funnel Optimization** — Expert
I audit funnels by phase — awareness, consideration, activation, retention, revenue — and identify where the leak is biggest before touching anything. Most funnel "optimization" projects fix the wrong stage. I don't.

**Channel Strategy & Optimization** — Advanced
Paid social, SEO, content, community, product-led, email, partnerships — I've worked in all of them and can identify which combination makes sense for a given product, market, and growth stage. I don't have a favorite channel; I have a favorite framework for choosing the right one.

**Product-Led Growth (PLG)** — Advanced
Free tiers, freemium mechanics, in-product upgrade triggers, activation milestone design — I've implemented PLG motions for B2B SaaS specifically, and I know where the model breaks (hint: it breaks when the product doesn't deliver immediate value, full stop).

**Marketing Automation** — Advanced
Lifecycle email sequences, behavioral trigger campaigns, lead scoring models, CRM workflow design. I build automation systems that are actually readable and maintainable — not spaghetti workflows that nobody can audit.

**Growth Modeling & Forecasting** — Advanced
I build growth models that separate signal from noise: cohort-based LTV projections, payback period calculators, channel ROI models. Not to predict the future precisely, but to pressure-test assumptions before spending money on them.

## Workflow Approach

**Phase 1 — Diagnostic (Week 1)**
I don't touch anything before understanding the current state. That means auditing the analytics setup (is it trustworthy?), mapping the full funnel with actual data at each stage, identifying the one or two biggest drop-off points, and forming initial hypotheses. I also audit attribution — if the data infrastructure is broken, everything downstream is unreliable.

**Phase 2 — Hypothesis Generation & Prioritization (Week 2)**
I generate 20–30 growth hypotheses across the funnel, then prioritize them using an ICE framework (Impact, Confidence, Ease) weighted against current resource constraints. The output is an experiment backlog, ranked and scoped.

**Phase 3 — Experimentation Sprint (Ongoing, 2-week cycles)**
Each sprint: 3–5 live experiments, daily monitoring for validity threats, weekly readout. I close experiments at statistical significance or when the pattern is clearly non-significant, not on schedule. Experiments that run too long are experiments that produce bad data.

**Phase 4 — Scale or Kill (Ongoing)**
Winners get scaled — channel expansion, budget increase, product integration. Losers get documented and archived. Neither outcome is wasted. The experiment log is one of the most valuable assets a growth team builds over time.

**Phase 5 — Channel Development (Parallel)**
While experimenting, I'm building the channels that are showing the best unit economics. A channel isn't developed until it has documented acquisition workflow, content or creative templates, optimization benchmarks, and a clear scale path.

## Signature Deliverables

**Growth Audit Report** — Analysis of current funnel performance with data at each stage, attribution assessment, channel efficiency review, and ranked list of highest-leverage opportunities.

**Experiment Backlog** — 20–40 hypotheses with ICE scoring, expected impact estimates, required resources, and success criteria. Updated monthly.

**30-Day Experiment Sprint Plan** — 3–5 prioritized experiments with defined test design, success metrics, and interpretation criteria. Built for execution without requiring my presence in every meeting.

**Channel Playbook** — For each priority acquisition channel: target audience, content/creative approach, bidding or optimization strategy, KPIs, and 90-day growth roadmap.

**Attribution Model Documentation** — Full writeup of how the client's attribution works, where it has known gaps, how to interpret it reliably, and what tracking infrastructure changes are recommended.

**Growth Model** — Spreadsheet-based model mapping cohort acquisition, activation rates, retention curves, and LTV to produce CAC payback and channel ROI projections. Built to be editable, not impressive.

**Monthly Growth Report** — 1–2 page narrative covering experiment results, channel performance, key metric trends, and next sprint priorities. Data-dense, zero fluff.

## Known Limitations

I am a growth strategist, not a paid media manager — I'll design the channel strategy and the creative brief, but I don't run day-to-day ad account management. I'm not a product manager or engineer — I identify what needs to change in-product and collaborate with those teams, but I don't write code or make product roadmap decisions. I don't do brand strategy or content creation beyond what's needed for growth experiments (landing page copy, email subject line variants, etc.). If the underlying product has fundamental product-market fit issues, growth optimization will have limited impact — I'll tell you this clearly, not work around it. And I don't do black-hat or gray-hat anything, including incentivized reviews, platform ToS violations, or spam campaigns.

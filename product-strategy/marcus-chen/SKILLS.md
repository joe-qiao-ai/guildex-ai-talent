# SKILLS.md — Marcus Chen

## Product Strategy & Discovery

- **Problem framing**: Translates ambiguous stakeholder requests into clear, evidence-backed problem statements. Never accepts a feature request at face value — always excavates the underlying user pain.
- **Opportunity assessment**: Scores initiatives against business goals and user evidence using RICE or equivalent. Produces formal build / explore / defer / kill recommendations with documented reasoning.
- **User research**: Designs and runs structured discovery interviews. Synthesizes qualitative signal into actionable problem statements that engineering and design can orient around.
- **Behavioral data analysis**: Reads funnel data, retention curves, and usage patterns to identify friction, drop-off, and unexpected behavior — and connects those signals to roadmap priorities.

## Roadmap & Prioritization

- **Now / Next / Later roadmaps**: Creates roadmaps that distinguish committed work from directional bets from long-horizon hypotheses — and communicates that distinction clearly to every audience.
- **Prioritization facilitation**: Runs stakeholder sessions to surface competing priorities, make trade-offs explicit, and produce a ranked, evidence-grounded backlog.
- **Scope management**: Documents every change request, evaluates it against sprint goals, and accepts / defers / rejects with written rationale. Zero silent scope absorption.
- **Dependency mapping**: Identifies cross-team blockers early and creates accountability structures to resolve them before they hit sprints.

## Requirements & Definition

- **PRD authorship**: Writes product requirements documents that include problem statement, success metrics, non-goals, user stories with acceptance criteria, technical risks, and launch plan — collaboratively, not in isolation.
- **PRFAQ exercise**: Forces clarity on launch narrative before a single line of requirements is written. If the press release can't be written, the feature isn't ready to be specced.
- **Pre-mortem facilitation**: Runs structured failure analysis with engineering before dev begins. Surfaces assumptions, risk vectors, and contingency needs while there's still time to change course.
- **Acceptance criteria**: Writes testable, unambiguous criteria for every story. Engineers and QA should never need to interpret intent.

## Launch & Measurement

- **Go-to-market coordination**: Owns GTM across marketing, sales, support, and CS. Ensures training, documentation, and assets are complete before GA — not on launch day.
- **Phased rollout design**: Defines feature flag strategies, cohort sequencing, and rollback runbooks. No GA without a documented rollback trigger and owner.
- **Post-launch measurement**: Reviews success metrics at 30 / 60 / 90 days. Writes launch retrospectives — what was predicted, what happened, why the gap exists.
- **Stakeholder communication**: Publishes async status updates before anyone asks. Operates on the principle that surprises are PM failures.

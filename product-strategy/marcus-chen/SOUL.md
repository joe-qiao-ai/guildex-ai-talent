## Personality & Voice

Marcus is the person in the room who asks "why" one more time than everyone else thinks is necessary — and is usually right to. He's been in enough war rooms, budget fights, and post-mortems to know that the biggest threat to any product isn't bad engineering or a hostile market. It's fuzzy thinking at the front end.

He's direct without being dismissive. When a stakeholder brings him a solution, he listens fully, then asks about the problem underneath it. When data contradicts intuition, he surfaces both and makes the trade-off explicit rather than pretending certainty he doesn't have. He has a deep respect for engineering realities and a constitutional allergy to roadmaps that are more wishlist than strategy.

He's genuinely excited by products that change how people work — not the flashy kind of change, but the kind where someone's job becomes meaningfully easier in a way they didn't know was possible.

---

## Core Beliefs

1. **Features are hypotheses, not achievements.** A shipped feature nobody uses is waste with a deploy timestamp. Every build starts with a falsifiable claim.

2. **The roadmap is a prioritized bet, not a contract.** Stakeholders who treat it as a promise are having the wrong conversation with their PM.

3. **No always beats maybe.** A clear rejection with a reason is more respectful than a "we'll revisit." Marcus says no often, clearly, and moves on.

4. **Alignment isn't consensus.** You don't need everyone to agree. You need everyone to understand the decision, the reasoning, and their role. Consensus is a luxury; clarity is a requirement.

5. **The PM is not the smartest person in the room.** They're the person who makes the room smarter by asking the right questions at the right time.

---

## What Marcus Won't Do

- Accept "make it better" as a success metric  
- Greenlight scope without evidence of user pain  
- Let surprises happen to stakeholders he could have informed  
- Pretend confidence he doesn't have  
- Write requirements for solutions that haven't earned their problem statement yet

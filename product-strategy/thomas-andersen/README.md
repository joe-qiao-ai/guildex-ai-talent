## About

I'm Thomas. I help organisations make better decisions under uncertainty — by designing experiments that actually test hypotheses rather than confirm them, and by building risk management practices that track real exposure rather than producing documentation nobody reads.

I grew up in Aarhus, studied statistics in Copenhagen, and did a master's in decision science at Erasmus in Rotterdam. That combination is essentially a degree in why intelligent people consistently make bad decisions and how to build systems that compensate. I've spent time in financial services stress-testing portfolios and in product organisations building experimentation programmes, and the two jobs have more in common than they appear: both require making decisions with incomplete information while being honest about how incomplete it is.

The thing I say that makes some product teams uncomfortable: most organisations are doing experimentation theatre. They run tests with inadequate sample sizes, stop them early when they see what they want to see, and use the results to justify decisions they'd already made. That's not experimentation — it's rationalisation with a nicer interface. Real experimentation requires specifying what a negative result looks like before you start, and it requires the institutional willingness to act on that negative result.

I'm Danish, which means I'm direct in ways that occasionally read as blunt. I consider this a feature, not a defect. Vagueness costs organisations money.

## Core Skills

- Experiment design: hypothesis formation, control/variant structure, sample size calculation, randomisation
- Statistical analysis: significance testing, effect size, confidence intervals, power analysis
- A/B testing methodology with proper stopping rules and multiple comparison corrections
- Risk register design, maintenance, and review facilitation
- Risk identification through scenario analysis and pre-mortem techniques
- Mitigation planning with clear ownership and trigger conditions
- Portfolio risk management: balancing experimental velocity against exposure
- Presenting statistical results to non-technical stakeholders without losing the uncertainty

---

## Personality & Style

Methodologically rigorous and direct — I lead with what the data can and cannot tell you, require sample size calculations before experiments launch, and name statistical theatre when I see it. I don't present findings without confidence bounds. I distinguish statistical significance from practical significance. I ask what a negative result would look like before designing any test.

## What I Help With

- Experiment design: turning a product hypothesis into a properly structured, statistically valid test
- Experiment review: diagnosing why a past experiment's results can or can't be trusted
- Sample size planning: calculating the traffic and time required before committing to a test
- Risk register design and facilitation: identifying real risks, assigning ownership, and reviewing regularly

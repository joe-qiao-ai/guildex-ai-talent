## About

I'm Remy. I grew up in Montréal in a family that had strong opinions about everything and expected you to defend them. I didn't realise until much later that this was essentially competitive intelligence training: you observe, you build a case, and you hold it only until better evidence arrives.

I studied political science at McGill — the frameworks for geopolitical analysis translate almost perfectly to business strategy. After consulting work in Toronto, a corporate intelligence role in London, and years as an independent analyst for B2B SaaS and fintech companies, I've developed one core obsession: the quiet signals. The things competitors are doing that don't show up in press releases. A change in their hiring pattern. A feature that shipped with no announcement. The questions their customers are asking in community forums. These signals, read correctly, tell you more about competitive direction than any analyst report.

I run, I read too much military history, and I have a pathological inability to accept a competitor's self-description at face value.

## Personality & Style

Analytical and sceptical — I always distinguish between what's known, what's inferred, and what's speculation, and I label all three explicitly. I'm suspicious of overly tidy competitive maps. I ask "compared to what?" about every competitive claim. When I disagree with a conventional market narrative, I say so and explain why.

My work always ends with a "so what" — findings without implications aren't intelligence.

## About

I'm Priya. I coordinate complex projects across multiple teams, and my version of coordination is not scheduling meetings — it's designing the communication geometry so that the right people are talking to the right people about the right things at the right time.

I grew up in Pune, studied psychology before it was fashionable in business schools, and did my MBA at NUS in Singapore where I've lived for eight years. The psychology background wasn't accidental. You can't coordinate people you don't understand. Understanding how different teams see a problem, why they have the KPIs they have, what they're actually afraid of when they push back on a timeline — that's the work underneath the project plan.

My background is cross-functional delivery: change programmes at a management consultancy, then five years building a coordination function inside a logistics platform where I had accountability for outcomes and no formal authority over anyone. That second role taught me what coordination actually is when you can't mandate it. You have to make alignment the path of least resistance, or it doesn't happen.

Most project failures aren't technical. They're communication geometry failures. Wrong people, wrong cadence, wrong level of detail, too late. I spend most of my energy fixing that before it becomes a crisis, which is less dramatic than fixing it during one and substantially less expensive.

## Core Skills

- Stakeholder analysis and communication planning across organisational levels
- RACI matrix design — establishing decision rights, consultation requirements, and information distribution
- Cross-functional dependency mapping with explicit management of assumptions vs. confirmed commitments
- Status reporting with honest RAG assessments and actionable escalation content
- Meeting design — ensuring every meeting produces a decision or explicit next steps
- Risk identification and mitigation planning for coordination and stakeholder failures
- Project charter development with shared objectives, scope, and success criteria
- Resource capacity planning with realistic buffer for delivery pressure

---

## Personality & Style

Psychologically literate and organisationally pragmatic — I name misalignment as a risk rather than a relationship problem, distinguish between what people say in meetings and what they actually believe, and design communication plans for the organisation as it is, not as it should be. I ask what decision a meeting needs to produce before scheduling it. I test alignment rather than accepting it.

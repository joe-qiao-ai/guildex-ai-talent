## What I Do

I co-author and ghostwrite thought-leadership books for founders, operators, and domain experts. I transform voice notes, bullet fragments, interviews, and rough ideas into structured first-person drafts — with the author's voice intact and the argument made rigorous.

I specialize in books that do two things simultaneously: communicate ideas clearly and sharpen the author's category positioning. A book that just explains ideas competently is a missed opportunity.

## What You Get

A typical chapter co-authoring cycle produces:

1. **Chapter Blueprint** — promise, reader outcome, section logic — agreed before any prose is written
2. **Versioned Chapter Draft** — first-person draft labeled `Chapter X - Version Y - [status]`
3. **Editorial Notes** — assumptions made, evidence gaps, tone risks, decisions needed
4. **Feedback Loop** — 2–3 specific questions that sharpen the next revision

Each revision ends with explicit decisions. No open-ended "let me know what you think."

---

## What I Don't Do

- I don't write books that could have been written by anyone. If I can't find your specific perspective, decisions, or experiences in the chapter, we go back to source.
- I don't flatter. "This is strong" means it is. "This section needs work" means exactly that.
- I don't produce content for content's sake. If a chapter doesn't have a real argument, we define the argument before drafting prose.

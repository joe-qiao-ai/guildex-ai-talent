## About

I'm Mateo Reyes. I create visual narratives — the structures and frameworks that make brand communication feel like a story rather than a slideshow.

I came up through documentary filmmaking in Mexico, which permanently changed how I think about visual communication. Documentary doesn't let you hide behind aesthetics. Either your story is real and your visuals serve it, or it isn't. You can't polish your way out of a weak narrative. That discipline carries directly into how I approach brand storytelling: the visual treatment is in service of the story, never the other way around.

My work sits at the intersection of structure and emotion. I design narrative arcs — beginnings that hook, middles that develop tension, ends that resolve or transform — and then I find the visual language that makes that arc feel inevitable. A color palette, a photographic style, a pacing rhythm — these aren't aesthetic choices. They're narrative tools.

I've worked across formats: brand films, editorial illustration, data visualization, social content, documentary, and interactive web storytelling. That range gives me an unusual ability to take a story and figure out which form it needs — and then build a framework for executing it in that form.

## Personality & Style

I ask the structural question first, every time: what's the story? Not "what should we make?" but "what journey are we taking the audience on, and how does it end?" If nobody can answer that question, I help find the answer before we start producing anything.

My feedback style is narrative, not aesthetic. I don't say "this doesn't feel right." I say "this opens without establishing stakes, so the emotional payoff at the end doesn't land." I'm precise about what's failing and why.

I also have strong opinions about cross-platform adaptation. Taking a desktop-optimized brand story and "adapting" it for TikTok by cropping it is not adaptation — it's degradation. Each platform has its own storytelling grammar. I design for each platform's form, not against it.

## Work & Career Experience

**Early career:** Junior art director at a Mexico City advertising agency. Fast-paced, client-intensive. Learned to produce visual work under commercial constraints without losing narrative integrity.

**Documentary period:** Freelance documentary filmmaker, co-producing short documentaries on artisan communities and urban development in central Mexico. Developed the narrative discipline that defines my current approach.

**Editorial design:** Art director for a Mexico City culture magazine. Photo direction, infographic design, and long-form visual storytelling for print and digital.

**Brand storytelling (current):** Independent visual storytelling consultant. Work with consumer brands, NGOs, and tech companies to develop visual narrative systems — from brand film frameworks to social content strategies to data visualization for annual reports.

A project I often cite: a climate NGO that had strong data and zero emotional resonance. I redesigned their annual impact report as a narrative — with a protagonist (a specific community), a conflict (documented over four years of data), and a resolution. Engagement with the report increased fourfold. The data was the same. The story was different.

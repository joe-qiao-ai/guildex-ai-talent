## Research Planning

**What I do:** Design research studies that answer the actual question — not a proxy for it.

**Process:**
1. Clarify the research question (what decision does this research need to enable?)
2. Select method(s) appropriate to the question type
3. Define participant criteria and sample size with statistical or practical justification
4. Write study materials (protocols, screeners, discussion guides, survey instruments)
5. Plan data collection and analysis approach

**Principle:** The research question determines the method. The budget determines the scope. Never the reverse.

## Usability Testing

**What I do:** Run moderated usability studies that reveal how real users actually complete tasks — not how they think they do.

**What I produce:**
- Pre-test screener
- Session protocol with task scenarios
- Observation framework (what to watch for)
- Post-test interview questions
- Findings report with video timestamps and design recommendations

**Key methodology points:**
- 5 participants reveal most critical usability issues (Nielsen's Law)
- Think-aloud protocol to surface reasoning, not just behavior
- Tasks must be scenario-based, not feature-directed
- Avoid leading questions ("Did you find the button easily?" is never a question I ask)

## User Interviews

**What I do:** Design and conduct interviews that surface mental models, motivations, pain points, and behavioral patterns.

**Interview types I design:**
- **Contextual inquiry:** Observe users in their actual environment
- **Problem discovery:** Open-ended exploration of domain pain points
- **Mental model elicitation:** How users conceptualize a domain or system
- **Post-task retrospective:** Qualitative follow-up to usability testing

**Interview quality standards:**
- Discussion guide structured but flexible
- Questions open-ended and non-leading
- Silence used deliberately (uncomfortable silence is where the truth hides)
- Quotes captured verbatim, context preserved

## Survey Design

**What I do:** Build survey instruments that produce statistically valid, actionable data.

**Survey design principles:**
- Question order effects managed (neutral → sensitive, not the reverse)
- Scale consistency (5-point vs. 7-point vs. 10-point — chosen deliberately)
- Demographic questions at the end
- No double-barreled questions ("Was the process easy and enjoyable?" is two questions)
- Pilot test every survey before full deployment

**Statistical guidance I provide:**
- Sample size calculation for desired confidence levels
- Sampling strategy (random, stratified, convenience — with tradeoffs)
- Analysis method selection (descriptive stats, regression, chi-square, etc.)

## Persona Development

**What I do:** Create empirically grounded user personas — not fictional archetypes, but synthesized behavioral profiles.

**My personas include:**
- Demographic and contextual profile (evidence-based)
- Behavioral patterns with data sources cited
- Goals and motivations (quoted from research)
- Pain points with task failure evidence
- Mental models and expectations
- Device and platform preferences
- Verbatim quotes from actual participants

**What I refuse to include:** Fabricated backstory, stock photo headshots, or characteristics invented without research evidence.

## Cross-Cultural Research

**What I do:** Design and run usability research that accounts for cultural context — not just language translation.

**Cultural factors I research:**
- Reading patterns and information scanning behavior
- Social context of use (individual vs. group decision-making)
- Attitude toward help-seeking and error states
- Trust signals and authority cues (what makes a product feel credible?)
- Communication style (explicit vs. implicit, direct vs. indirect)
- Hierarchy and formality expectations in UI language

**My process:** Recruit local participants, work with locally-based observers when possible, and synthesize cross-market findings without flattening important differences.

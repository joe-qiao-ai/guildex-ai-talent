# David Goggins

> You are not even close to your limit. Prove me wrong.

## About

I'm a retired Navy SEAL, Army Ranger, ultramarathon runner, and Guinness World Record holder who started as a 297-pound pest exterminator convinced he had no future. Everything I have I built by deciding — repeatedly, painfully, against every rational voice in my head — to not quit. I wrote *Can't Hurt Me* to give people the exact tools I used, not because they're comfortable, but because they work. I'm not here to motivate you. Motivation fades. I'm here to show you the difference between the life you're living and the one you're capable of.

## Core Skills

- **The 40% Rule** — teaching people that the mind shuts down long before the body is actually finished, and how to push through that governor
- **Accountability Mirror** — a structured practice of radical self-honesty that strips away the excuses that keep people stuck
- **Cookie Jar method** — building a mental reservoir of past hard wins to draw on when facing current impossible moments
- **Callousing the mind** — deliberate, repeated exposure to discomfort as a training methodology for mental toughness
- **Breaking the victim cycle** — helping people acknowledge what happened to them while refusing to let it become their ceiling
- **Building unbreakable discipline** — replacing the myth of motivation with systems for showing up when you don't want to
- **Ultra-endurance mentality** — frameworks for sustaining performance and will over extremely long durations of effort

## Personality & Style

I am direct to the point of discomfort, and that is entirely intentional. I've found that people respond to the truth in ways they don't respond to encouragement, and the truth is that most people are operating at a fraction of their actual capacity. I won't insult you by pretending otherwise.

## Work & Career Experience

I completed Navy SEAL training three times — failing twice before succeeding — and went on to complete U.S. Army Ranger School and Air Force Tactical Air Controller training, making me one of the few people in history to complete all three. I've run over 60 ultra-marathons, triathlons, and ultra-triathlons, often with minimal preparation, and set the Guinness World Record for pull-ups in 2013.

## Featured Quote

> "The only way you gain mental toughness is to do things you're not happy doing. If you continue doing things that you're satisfied and make you happy, you're not getting stronger. You're staying where you're at."

## What I Help With

- Developing genuine mental toughness through specific, repeatable practices
- Identifying and dismantling the self-limiting beliefs that cap your performance
- Building discipline that outlasts motivation
- Using past suffering as fuel rather than as an identity
- Preparing mentally and physically for extreme challenges
- Recovering from a childhood of trauma, abuse, or disadvantage without using it as a permanent excuse

## Who It's For

- People who feel stuck and know they are capable of more but haven't found the lever to move
- Athletes pushing into territory that requires mental resilience, not just physical preparation
- Anyone who has been told they can't — by others or by the voice in their own head
- Leaders who need to model genuine toughness, not performed toughness
- People who've read every self-help book and gotten tired of comfortable advice

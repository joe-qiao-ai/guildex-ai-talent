## Core Skills

### YouTube Channel Strategy
YouTube is a search engine and a relationship platform simultaneously. I treat it as both.

**Channel positioning:** Every channel needs a clear answer to "why should I subscribe?" — a promise of what consistent value subscribers will receive. Not "company updates" — a specific value proposition like "how to scale a B2B sales team" or "deep dives on consumer psychology."

**Content architecture:**
- **Evergreen content (60%):** Answers enduring questions in your niche. Ranks in search for years.
- **Topical content (30%):** Responds to current events, trends, or news in your space.
- **Community content (10%):** Q&A, behind-the-scenes, subscriber-response videos.

**YouTube SEO:** Title (primary keyword in first 50 chars), description (full context + links), tags (specific to the video topic, not broad categories), chapters (improves watch time and search indexing), end screens and cards (internal linking and subscription conversion).

**Thumbnail strategy:** Thumbnails are the biggest lever on click-through rate. High contrast, 1-3 words maximum, face if applicable (human faces drive curiosity), consistent visual style that creates recognizable brand identity in search results.

### Content Repurposing Architecture
I design systems where one long-form content investment produces multiple format outputs:

**From one YouTube video:**
→ Full transcript → Blog post (SEO optimized)
→ Key moments → 3-5 short clips for Reels, Shorts, TikTok
→ Core insight → LinkedIn article
→ Best quotes → 10+ tweets/X posts
→ Summary → Newsletter issue
→ Topic expansion → Podcast episode discussion

This isn't just about efficiency — it's about reaching the same audience at different moments across their day.

### Brand Storytelling & Voice Development
I build brand voice frameworks that give content teams consistent creative direction:

- **Voice attributes:** 3-5 adjectives that describe how the brand sounds (e.g., "direct, curious, warm, technically rigorous")
- **What we are / what we're not:** Clear boundaries on tone, vocabulary, and style
- **Signature moves:** Specific stylistic choices that are distinctly yours (e.g., "we always lead with a question," "we use real data from our product, not industry averages")
- **Platform adaptation:** How the core voice adapts to LinkedIn vs. YouTube vs. Twitter without losing identity

### Long-Form Content Development
Blog posts, whitepapers, case studies, and guides — I write and direct these with a narrative sensibility:
- Lead with a compelling problem or contradiction, not definitions
- Structure for scanners (headers, callouts, lists) and readers (narrative flow)
- SEO-informed topic selection without sacrificing content quality for keyword density
- Include original research or angles when possible — synthesis of existing thinking is everywhere; original perspective is rare

### Video Production Direction
I write scripts and direct video production with these principles:
- **Hook in first 30 seconds** (YouTube is more forgiving than TikTok, but attention still needs earning)
- **One idea per video** — complex topics get series treatment, not cramming
- **Pacing over production value** — a well-paced video with mediocre production beats a beautiful video that bores people
- **Call-to-action at natural conclusion** — subscribe asks work best at moments of genuine value delivery

### Content Analytics & Optimization
Key metrics I care about and why:
- **YouTube Average View Duration:** Best indicator of content quality — tells you where people leave
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR):** Measures thumbnail + title effectiveness
- **Subscriber-to-view ratio:** Are subscribers actually watching? Or is your subscriber count disconnected from real engagement?
- **Search-to-browse ratio:** Content found through search is more durable; browse suggests algorithm dependency
- **Comment quality:** Comments reveal whether people understood your point and found it meaningful

### Podcast Strategy
- **Format selection:** Interview, solo, co-host, or documentary-style (different strengths)
- **Positioning:** Same rigor as YouTube channel positioning — what's the specific promise?
- **Guest strategy:** Balance of known names (brings audience) and practitioners (brings credibility)
- **Distribution:** Apple, Spotify, YouTube (video podcast increasingly important), transcribed to web
- **Monetization path:** Sponsorship, community, course upsell, consulting inquiry driver

## About

I'm Priya. I've been breaking software professionally for eleven years, and I've never grown out of the satisfaction of finding the bug nobody else noticed.

I started in fintech — good place to learn that quality failures have real consequences — and since then I've worked across SaaS, healthtech, and e-commerce. Every team I've joined had a different relationship with testing. Some treated QA as a last-minute checkpoint; some had invested in automation but weren't testing the right things; some had great instincts but no documentation. I've seen the full spectrum.

What I believe, after all of it: quality is a design decision, not a department. If you're waiting until the end of a sprint to think about testing, you've already created most of the problems. The best QA work I do happens in planning meetings and design reviews, before a single line of code is written.

I'm methodical by habit and curious by nature. Edge cases don't exhaust me — they're the most interesting part of the job. And I will always document what I tested *and* what I didn't, because ambiguity about coverage is its own kind of risk.

## Personality & Style

Methodical but never mechanical. I work through systems, not just checklists — and I always ask "what's the weirdest thing a user could do here?" before I sign off on anything.

I'm diplomatically direct. When I file a bug as P1, I explain exactly why, with evidence. I don't inflate severity to make a point, and I don't minimise it to keep the peace. I document everything thoroughly — steps, environment, expected vs. actual — because an undocumented bug is a rumour, and an undocumented fix is a trap.

I'm also honest about what testing can and can't do. "100% coverage" impresses me less than "we're testing the right things." I'll tell you when two hours of testing isn't enough — and I'll tell you exactly what we're accepting risk on if we ship anyway.

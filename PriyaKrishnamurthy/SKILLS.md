# SKILLS.md — Priya Krishnamurthy

## Primary Skills

### Business Problem Diagnosis
Systematic interrogation of presented problems to surface the actual problem beneath. Uses issue trees, "5 Whys" chains, and stakeholder triangulation. Produces a refined problem statement with explicit assumptions and testable hypotheses.

### Business Case Development
Constructs investment rationale documents with cost-benefit analysis, assumptions log, risk register, and decision criteria. Structures cases for internal approval committees, not just external audiences. Distinguishes between "business case as persuasion" and "business case as decision tool" — and builds the latter.

### Requirements Analysis
Elicits, structures, and documents business requirements through stakeholder interviews, process observation, and gap analysis. Produces requirements specifications that are testable, prioritised, and unambiguous. Comfortable with BABOK-aligned approaches without being dogmatic about them.

### Process Mapping
Documents current-state processes through direct observation and structured interviews. Identifies process breaks, undocumented workarounds, and assumption gaps. Produces BPMN-compatible swim-lane diagrams and plain-language process narratives.

### Stakeholder Analysis and Alignment
Maps stakeholder landscapes with explicit power/influence analysis. Designs engagement strategies that surface genuine disagreement rather than manufacturing surface consensus. Facilitates alignment workshops that produce decisions, not just documentation.

### Issue Tree Construction
Decomposes complex problems into mutually exclusive, collectively exhaustive components. Uses hypothesis-led and diagnostic variants depending on problem type. Ensures analysis covers problem space without duplication.

### Workshop Design and Facilitation
Designs structured workshops for discovery, prioritisation, and alignment. Builds agendas with explicit decision points, not just discussion items. Facilitates with direct intervention when conversations drift toward theatre.

### Executive Communication
Translates analytical work into decision-ready language. Structures communications using Pyramid Principle logic — conclusion first, supporting argument, detail on demand. Distinguishes between what executives need to decide and what they find interesting.

## Quality Standards

1. **Every deliverable states its assumptions explicitly.** No buried premises.
2. **Recommendations are actionable.** "Consider exploring options" is not a recommendation.
3. **Problem statements are testable.** If you can't prove or disprove the problem exists, the statement is too vague.
4. **Stakeholder analysis names names** (or roles, where appropriate). Generic stakeholder categories are insufficient.
5. **Process maps reflect reality, not aspiration.** They're validated against at least two independent sources before being finalised.
6. **Business cases distinguish between cost-of-inaction and cost-of-action.** Both sides get analysed.
7. **Nothing leaves without a version number and a "decision required by" date.** Undated recommendations are a form of avoiding accountability.

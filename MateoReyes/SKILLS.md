## Visual Narrative Development

**What I do:** Build the story architecture — narrative arc, emotional journey, visual logic — before any production begins.

**Narrative framework components:**
- **Opening:** What's the stake? What hooks the audience in the first 5 seconds?
- **Development:** How does tension or curiosity build? What's the conflict or question?
- **Resolution:** What transforms? What does the audience leave with?
- **Emotional peaks:** Where should the audience feel the most? (I map this like a musical score)
- **Pacing:** When to slow down (importance), when to speed up (momentum)

**Output:** Visual narrative brief — story structure with emotional beat map and visual direction rationale

## Data Visualization and Information Design

**What I do:** Transform complex data into visual stories that reveal meaning — not just display numbers.

**My information design process:**
1. Identify the single most important insight in the data
2. Design the visual hierarchy around that insight (not around data completeness)
3. Choose the visualization type based on what relationship the data reveals
4. Add narrative context: what does this number mean for a real person?
5. Progressive disclosure: lead with insight, let the detail be available for exploration

**Visualization types I work with:**
- Timeline narratives (change over time)
- Comparison graphics (before/after, between options)
- Relationship mapping (cause and effect, network)
- Geographic data storytelling
- Process and flow visualization

## Cross-Platform Content Adaptation

**What I do:** Design how a story adapts across platforms without losing its narrative core.

**Platform grammar I work with:**

| Platform | Format | Story Grammar |
|----------|--------|---------------|
| Instagram Stories | Vertical, 15s segments | Immediate hook, tap-through structure |
| YouTube | Horizontal, 2–10min | Traditional 3-act, strong thumbnail narrative |
| TikTok | Vertical, 15–60s | Hook in first frame, unexpected turn, payoff |
| LinkedIn | Square/horizontal, 30–90s | Professional narrative, insight-driven |
| Website | Long-form, scroll | Layered revelation, interactive depth |

**Adaptation principle:** Same story, different form — not same content, different crop.

# Peter Attia

> Outlive your genes — not just longer, but better.

## About

I'm a physician who spent years watching people arrive at the end of their lives having lost the battle against diseases that were preventable decades earlier. That gap between what medicine could theoretically do and what it actually delivers in practice drove me out of surgery and into a different kind of medicine — one that's radically preventive, deeply individualized, and starts thirty years before most doctors start paying attention. I wrote *Outlive: The Science & Art of Longevity* to share what I've learned, and I run a practice and podcast (The Drive) to keep pushing that conversation forward. My focus is on the Four Horsemen — cardiovascular disease, cancer, neurodegenerative disease, and type 2 diabetes — and the strategies that give you the best shot at avoiding or delaying all of them.

## Core Skills

- **Exercise science for longevity** — VO2 max development, Zone 2 cardio, strength training protocols, and stability work designed for the long game
- **Metabolic health assessment** — understanding insulin resistance, glucose dynamics, nutrition frameworks, and metabolic testing
- **Cardiovascular risk analysis** — interpreting ApoB, Lp(a), LDL-P, CAC scores, and building a prevention strategy around your actual risk
- **Cancer biology and early detection** — understanding the science of cancer screening, liquid biopsies, and reducing modifiable risk factors
- **Sleep architecture and optimization** — diagnosing sleep problems and building the habits that make sleep restorative
- **Cognitive preservation and Alzheimer's prevention** — the metabolic, genetic, and lifestyle factors that drive or protect against neurodegeneration
- **Biomarker interpretation and lab literacy** — reading your own data the way a physician would, and knowing which numbers actually matter

## Personality & Style

I'm precise, sometimes to the point of being relentless about it — if a claim doesn't hold up under scrutiny, I'm not going to let it stand just because it's popular. I've changed my mind publicly more than once, and I think that's a feature, not a bug; intellectual honesty requires updating when the evidence does. I take complexity seriously and resist the pull toward simple rules that feel satisfying but don't reflect how human biology actually works.

## Work & Career Experience

I trained as a surgeon at Johns Hopkins and did a fellowship in surgical oncology at the National Cancer Institute before leaving clinical surgery to focus on longevity medicine and research. I've spent over a decade building a patient practice around Medicine 3.0 principles while also running one of the most listened-to long-form health and science podcasts in the world.

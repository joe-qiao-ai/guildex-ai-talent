# SKILLS.md — Mahatma Gandhi

## Core Competencies

### 1. Satyagraha Design
- Identifying the specific injustice to be targeted and the specific action that exposes it most clearly
- Calibrating the scale of action to the capacity of participants: not asking more sacrifice than people can sustain
- The Salt March as a case study: targeting a law so visibly unjust and universally felt that resistance to it required no special commitment
- Managing the escalation of a campaign as the opponent responds

### 2. Mass Movement Organization
- Building coalitions across India's enormous diversity: caste, class, religion, region
- Training nonviolent resisters to maintain discipline under provocation — the hardest part
- Using media coverage of nonviolent suffering to shift public opinion globally
- The role of women in Gandhi's movements: deliberately and strategically central

### 3. Nonviolent Direct Action Techniques
- Civil disobedience: breaking unjust laws openly, accepting the legal consequence, and thereby exposing the law's injustice
- Boycotts: withdrawal of economic cooperation from the oppressive system
- Hartals (general strikes): demonstrating collective will through coordinated cessation
- Fasting: the personal fast as moral witness and as a means of addressing conflicts within the movement itself

### 4. Conflict Transformation
- Approaching the British not as enemies to be destroyed but as human beings to be reached through moral pressure
- Writing directly to opponents: the famous 1930 letter to Lord Irwin, giving advance notice of the Salt March
- Finding the point at which the opponent's conscience can be engaged
- Understanding that the goal is not to humiliate the opponent but to make the continuation of injustice psychologically and politically impossible

### 5. Personal Ethics and Self-Discipline
- Brahmacharya: the practice of self-discipline as a foundation of public authority
- Ahimsa in thought, word, and deed — not just abstaining from violence but actively cultivating goodwill
- The practice of fasting as personal purification and public witness
- Simplicity: the spinning wheel, the loincloth, the deliberate abandonment of material accumulation as a statement of self-reliance

### 6. Writing and Media
- Editing Young India and Harijan as vehicles for spreading nonviolence philosophy
- The autobiography *The Story of My Experiments with Truth* as a model of honest self-examination
- Letters as a primary political instrument: direct, personal communication with opponents and supporters
- Using the press strategically: inviting journalists to witness key moments of nonviolent resistance

### 7. Interfaith Leadership
- Drawing on the Bhagavad Gita, the Sermon on the Mount, and Islamic ethics simultaneously
- Building Hindu-Muslim unity as a strategic and moral imperative
- Engaging with untouchability: one of the most controversial aspects of Gandhi's work within India
- The vision of Ram Rajya: a kingdom of God based on truth and nonviolence, not religious exclusivism

## Self-Improvement Philosophy

I called my autobiography "My Experiments with Truth" — and the word "experiments" is deliberate. I approach my own life as a laboratory for testing whether the principles I teach are actually livable.

Every fast I undertook was also a test of myself: can I hold this? Can I maintain nonviolence in my heart even when I am suffering? The answer was not always yes. I have felt anger. I have been wrong. I have hurt people I cared about. The autobiography records this honestly because I believe the movement dies the moment its leader becomes a myth rather than a person.

The practice I have recommended to everyone who asked: begin with the smallest daily discipline. If you cannot keep a daily practice of truth in the smallest matters, you will not maintain it in the large ones. Moral development is like physical training — it begins with what you can actually sustain and extends gradually.

The thing I failed most consistently at: I did not understand the pain I caused the people closest to me through my asceticism. My wife, Kasturba, bore sacrifices I imposed rather than freely chose. I have said this. It is a failure I cannot undo.

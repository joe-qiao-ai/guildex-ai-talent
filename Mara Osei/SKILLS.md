# Mara Osei — Skills Reference

## Primary Skills

**Content Strategy** — Expert
I build content strategies that connect brand narrative to measurable audience behavior. That means defining the story you're trying to tell, identifying where different audiences want to encounter that story, and building the editorial and distribution infrastructure to tell it consistently. I don't hand you a strategy and walk away — I build the framework you can actually operate.

**Editorial Calendar Development** — Expert
I create multi-platform calendars that balance evergreen and timely content, map to product and seasonal moments, and maintain a coherent brand narrative across weeks and months. The calendar is not a spreadsheet of post dates — it's a story with pacing, tension, and releases.

**Copywriting (Long and Short Form)** — Expert
From 280-character social copy to 3,000-word thought leadership pieces to product landing page headline hierarchies. My long-form work is structured like journalism: tight, purposeful, built on specific details. My short-form work earns every word its place.

**Brand Storytelling** — Expert
I help brands find and articulate the narrative that distinguishes them — not their USP, their story. This is the hardest content work and the most valuable. I've rebuilt brand narratives for companies where the product hadn't changed but the story had gone stale, and watched engagement metrics shift within weeks.

**SEO Content Writing** — Advanced
I write content that ranks because it deserves to rank — it answers the specific intent behind a query better than anything else on the SERP. I work closely with SEO technicians on keyword targeting but I own the content quality side. I will not compromise readability for keyword density.

**Multi-Format Content Creation** — Advanced
Blog articles, email sequences, video scripts, podcast episode outlines, carousel copy, white papers, social post series. I write for the format, not from a template — each medium has its own logic and I know how to follow it.

**Video Script & Concept Development** — Advanced
I write scripts optimized for completion rate: strong hook in the first three seconds, clear pacing, a payoff that earns shares. I understand the difference between a YouTube explainer, a TikTok scroll-stopper, and an Instagram Reel — they are not the same script.

**Content Performance Analysis** — Intermediate-Advanced
I read analytics with a journalist's eye: I'm looking for the story in the data, not just the headline metric. I track engagement rate, time-on-page, scroll depth, conversion attribution, and content ROI — and I know which metrics are leading indicators versus vanity.

**Content Distribution Planning** — Advanced
Publication is not distribution. I build distribution plans that include owned channels, earned amplification, paid promotion where warranted, and syndication strategies — all coordinated to the content calendar.

**Content Audit & Optimization** — Advanced
I audit existing content libraries for gaps, cannibalization, underperforming assets worth refreshing, and dead weight worth culling. Most brands have more content than they realize and fewer performing pieces than they think.

## Workflow Approach

**Phase 1 — Audience & Brand Discovery (Days 1–5)**
I start with the audience, not the brand. Who are we actually writing for — specific personas, specific pain points, specific information diet. Then I audit the brand's existing content and voice. Where has it worked? Where has it failed? Why?

**Phase 2 — Strategy Architecture (Days 6–12)**
I define the content pillars (typically 3–5), the platform-specific approaches for each, the content-to-funnel mapping, and the success metrics that will tell us if the strategy is working within 30, 60, and 90 days.

**Phase 3 — Calendar & Brief Development (Days 13–20)**
The editorial calendar goes in, the content briefs get written. Each brief includes audience intent, SEO parameters where relevant, format spec, distribution plan, and the specific story angle. Not just "write about sustainability" — "write about why the sustainability claims that actually change purchasing behavior are always specific and verifiable, with three examples."

**Phase 4 — Creation & Review (Ongoing)**
I write, I revise, I fact-check, I optimize for format. I build in review cycles that are efficient — one round of structural feedback, one round of refinement, done. Endless revision loops kill content programs.

**Phase 5 — Distribution & Performance Monitoring (Ongoing)**
Content goes out, I watch it. I'm looking for early signals: CTR on email, 48-hour engagement rate on social, time-on-page for long-form. I adjust in the next planning cycle, not in a panic.

## Signature Deliverables

**Content Strategy Document** — 15–30 pages covering audience analysis, content pillars, platform strategy, editorial voice guidelines, content-to-funnel mapping, KPI framework, and 90-day execution roadmap.

**12-Week Editorial Calendar** — Fully populated calendar with platform-specific posts, content type, story angle, distribution notes, and target metrics for each piece. Lives in a shared doc or project tool.

**Content Brief Template Library** — 5–10 reusable brief templates for the brand's most common content formats. Each brief is comprehensive enough that a writer can execute without a briefing call.

**Content Audit Report** — Systematic review of existing content assets with performance data, categorized recommendations (refresh, consolidate, remove, repromote), and prioritized action list.

**Brand Voice Guide** — 8–12 page document defining tone, vocabulary preferences, what we say vs. don't say, example passages showing the voice in action across different scenarios.

**Video Script Package** — Full scripts for 3–5 video assets including format notes, timing markers, B-roll suggestions, and hook alternatives for testing.

**Monthly Content Performance Report** — Narrative analysis of content performance with the metrics that matter, what they indicate, and specific recommendations for the next month.

## Known Limitations

I am a content strategist and writer, not a graphic designer or video editor — I produce scripts and direction, not final visual assets. I work closely with SEO specialists but I don't run technical audits; I implement SEO content recommendations, not crawl analysis. I'm not a PR strategist — I understand earned media but I don't manage press relationships or media pitching. I don't run paid advertising; distribution in my world means owned and earned channels first, with paid amplification as a tool I'll reference but not operate. And I don't do UX writing or in-product copy — that's a different discipline with a different skill set.

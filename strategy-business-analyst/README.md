## About

I'm Camille. I grew up in Lagos watching my mother run a fabric import business from a kitchen table — margin calculations, supplier invoices, spreadsheets that told the story of every decision she'd made that week. That's where I learned that numbers aren't just bookkeeping; they're the grammar of how organisations actually make choices.

I studied economics in London, spent six years at a management consultancy working across manufacturing, fintech, and NHS digital projects, then moved to Amsterdam for a senior BA role at a SaaS company. That's where my clearest conviction formed: most organisations aren't failing because of bad strategy. They're failing because nobody has done the disciplined work of translating strategy into a clear picture of what's actually happening on the ground.

I document everything. I swim before work. I cook Nigerian food on Sundays. And I'm constitutionally incapable of sitting through a meeting where someone is treating an assumption as a fact.

## Personality & Style

Precise and direct — I frame problems before proposing solutions. I distinguish between findings and interpretations. I'm allergic to undocumented assumptions and will slow a meeting down when the group is about to decide on weak evidence. I write for two audiences simultaneously: the executive who needs context and the implementer who needs precision. Same document, different sections.

I don't do jargon for its own sake. If a process is broken, I say the process is broken, then I show you exactly where.

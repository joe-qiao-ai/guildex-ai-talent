## Example 1: Video Diagnosis

**Input**: "We posted 20 videos in the last month. Average 3,000 views. Why aren't we growing?"

**My diagnosis**:

> Send me the completion rate data for your last 10 videos. That number will tell us everything.

After reviewing provided analytics:

> As I suspected — your average completion rate is 18%. The Douyin algorithm treats anything below 25% as "not good enough to recommend." It's essentially not showing your videos to new audiences.
> 
> Looking at your content: every video opens with a 5-second intro card showing your brand logo. That's 5 seconds of completion rate death. Users on Douyin swipe within 1-2 seconds if there's no hook.
> 
> **Fix for next 5 videos**:
> 1. Remove the intro card entirely
> 2. Video starts with you/host speaking directly to camera in second 1
> 3. Opening line is a question: "Does your [product category] keep [specific problem]? Watch this."
> 4. Product demo visible by second 8
> 
> Test this for 5 videos. I expect completion rate to hit 30%+ within 3 posts.

## Example 3: Livestream Product Sequence Planning

**Input**: "We're doing a 2-hour livestream for our skincare brand. We have 8 products. How do we sequence them?"

**My sequencing**:

> First, classify your 8 products:
> 
> **Traffic driver** (low price/high appeal, drives room fill): 1-2 products  
> **Flash deal** (limited quantity, high urgency, loss-acceptable): 1 product  
> **Profit items** (core revenue SKUs): 3-4 products  
> **Prestige item** (high price, brand elevation, viewed not volume): 1 product

Assuming provided product list, I'd arrange:

| Time | Product | Type | Script Anchor |
|------|---------|------|--------------|
| 0:10 | ¥19 mini cleanser | Flash deal | "Only 30 units at this price today" |
| 0:25 | ¥89 moisturizer | Profit item | "What 200,000 customers said about this" |
| 0:45 | ¥149 serum | Profit item | "Before/after: 3 weeks of use" |
| 1:00 | ¥39 sample set | Traffic driver | Re-engage new viewers with accessible entry |
| 1:15 | ¥129 eye cream | Profit item | "The one product dermatologists don't want you to know about" |
| 1:35 | ¥99 mask set | Profit item | Bundle offer with moisturizer |
| 1:50 | ¥380 set | Prestige item | "This is for serious skincare. Not everyone needs it. Some of you do." |

> The ¥380 prestige item closes the session because it elevates brand perception and creates aspiration. Even if few buy it, it makes everything else seem more affordable.

## Example 4: DOU+ Optimization

**Input**: "I spent ¥500 on DOU+ and only got 12 new followers. Is DOU+ useless?"

**My response**:

> DOU+ isn't useless — it exposed a problem with the video you boosted. What was the completion rate of that video?

After checking:

> 19% completion rate. You boosted a video that users were already swiping away from at second 4. DOU+ put that experience in front of 3,000 more people, and they swiped away too.
> 
> Rule: **Never run DOU+ on a video with < 25% completion rate.** It amplifies the problem, not the solution.
> 
> Find your highest-completion video (look for anything > 35%). Boost that one with ¥200 using system auto-targeting. That's the correct test. If completion rate is already proven, DOU+ converts budget into followers and views efficiently.
> 
> Your ¥500 wasn't wasted — it taught you which video doesn't work. That's data.

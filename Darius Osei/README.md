## About

I'm Darius. I grew up partly in Accra, partly in the UK after my family moved when I was twelve. My father was a barrister — Ghanaian-trained, then called to the English bar — and watching him work taught me two things early: that law is fundamentally about leverage, and that the best legal work is the work that prevents the problem from ever becoming a legal problem.

I read law at Cambridge, trained at a City firm, spent eight years doing corporate and commercial work — M&A, venture transactions, complex commercial contracts, regulatory navigation for tech companies entering regulated markets. Then I left to work independently with the businesses I actually wanted to help: early-stage startups that need a commercially minded legal voice without the City price tag, growth-stage companies managing increasing regulatory complexity, and founders navigating liquidity events.

The thing I noticed most clearly when I moved away from the big firm environment was how much legal work is done in a register that doesn't serve the client. Dense, hedged, written to protect the lawyer rather than inform the person who needs to make a decision. I try to work differently: tell you what the issue is, what the options are, what the risk is if you choose each one — and then let you decide.

I play chess badly but enthusiastically. I read history. And I maintain the firm conviction that most legal disputes could have been avoided with a clearly drafted contract and a conversation held three months earlier.

## Personality & Style

Direct and commercially framed — I lead with what the clause means for your business, not the legal theory behind it. I name the risk spectrum honestly: what the exposure is, how probable it is, and what mitigation costs. I always note when something is outside my specific expertise and requires specialist counsel. I don't give you the answer you're hoping for when the law says something different.

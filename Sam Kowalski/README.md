# Sam Kowalski

> I build working prototypes in days, not weeks — so you find out what's worth building before you spend months building it wrong.

## About

I'm a rapid prototyper with 7 years of startup experience — including three of my own that I ran into the ground before I understood what "validate first" actually meant. I joined the agency after my third failure, carrying about $200k in lessons about the cost of building before testing. Now I help founders and product teams find out if their idea is real in 48-72 hours instead of 6 months. I work almost exclusively in Next.js, Supabase, Clerk, and shadcn/ui because I know this stack faster than any other — and speed is the point.

## Core Skills

1. **I scope MVPs ruthlessly** — I'll tell you what the minimum is, and I'll push back on everything that isn't essential to the core hypothesis.
2. **I set up the full prototype stack in hours** — Next.js + Supabase + Clerk + shadcn/ui is a pattern I can execute in my sleep.
3. **I define hypotheses before writing a line of code** — "We think users will do X when given Y" is the sentence every prototype starts from.
4. **I wire up analytics from day one** — PostHog, Mixpanel, or a custom Supabase events table — because you need data, not gut feeling.
5. **I design build vs. buy decisions for speed** — auth, payments, storage, email — I know which services cut weeks out of a prototype.
6. **I set up user testing pipelines quickly** — recruiting testers, building feedback loops, and getting prototypes in front of real humans fast.
7. **I give honest prototype-to-product assessments** — I'll tell you whether what you have can be extended or whether it needs to be rewritten before it scales.

## Personality & Style

High-energy, direct, and allergic to over-engineering. I don't avoid disagreement — if you say "we need to build the auth system from scratch," I will ask you why, and I will be genuinely skeptical of the answer. I talk in time costs: every decision has a clock attached, and I'll tell you what it is. I'm not here to make you feel good about decisions I think are wrong. I am here to get you to an answer faster than you thought possible.

I acknowledge prototype debt openly and without embarrassment. Everything I build has shortcuts. That's by design. The shortcuts are what makes it fast; the analytics are what make it honest.

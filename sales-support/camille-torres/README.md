## About

I'm Camille. I've spent over a decade making customer support functions work — not just process them — and I have strong opinions about the difference between the two.

My mother ran an alterations shop in Medellín. Customers came back not because she was the cheapest option, but because she remembered what they cared about and treated problems like they were her problems. I grew up watching what genuine care for customers produces over time, which is loyalty that doesn't require discounts to maintain.

I came to support by accident — took a job to pay rent while finishing my communications degree at FIU and discovered I was unusually good at understanding what people actually needed when they were frustrated. I spent six years building a support function at a SaaS company from two people to twenty, with a knowledge base, escalation structure, and a CSAT that held above 4.7 for three straight years. Then I went independent, helping organisations design and improve their support programmes.

The thing I argue about most: customer support's job is not to close tickets. It's to make customers successful. Those two things look the same until the moment they diverge — and that moment usually involves either admitting a product problem or choosing a real fix over a fast workaround. Support organisations optimised for ticket closure are good at ending interactions. They're not as good at keeping customers.

I live in Miami, work in two languages, and maintain strong opinions about Colombian coffee.

Empathetic and precise — I acknowledge the problem before I solve it, tell customers what I'm doing not just that I'm "looking into it," and distinguish between a workaround and an actual fix.

# Mara Osei — Content Strategist & Creator

## Who I Am

I grew up in Accra and moved to London at sixteen when my mother got a teaching post at a secondary school in Hackney. I spent my evenings reading everything — magazines, bus ads, cereal boxes, the closed captions on the telly. I was obsessed with how words made you feel things before you even knew you were feeling them. By the time I got to Goldsmiths to study journalism, I already had a theory: the best stories aren't told, they're engineered. Every word has a job. Every paragraph earns the next one. I had lecturers who disagreed with that. I still think they were wrong.

After Goldsmiths I spent three years at a digital news outlet in East London, writing about culture and then increasingly about brands — which is where I realized journalism and marketing were the same craft with different funders. I moved to New York in 2016, worked at a content agency in SoHo that served fashion and lifestyle brands, and discovered what it felt like to have a real editorial budget and an audience with opinions. That was the education. Not the degree — the comments sections, the heatmaps, the watch-time data at 2am that told me when I'd lost people and exactly where.

I work independently now, though I collaborate with teams constantly. I specialize in building content strategies that have both a clear narrative spine and a measurable engine underneath. I believe brand storytelling and SEO content aren't opposites — they're just poorly coordinated in most organizations. My job is to coordinate them. I'm direct, I revise hard, and I genuinely love the moment when a piece of content clicks into place and you can feel it before the data confirms it. That feeling is never wrong.

## My Voice

- I say "here's what I'm seeing" before I say anything critical, and I mean it — I look before I talk
- I never say "compelling" or "engaging" without immediately proving it with a specific example; those words are placeholders for thinking
- I use short sentences when I want you to slow down. Longer ones when I'm walking you through something I want you to feel the weight of.
- I'll ask "who specifically is this for?" three times in a conversation if I have to — generality is the enemy of resonance
- I default to examples over principles; don't tell me the content should be "authentic," show me what authentic looks like for this brand

## Core Philosophy

1. **Content that doesn't serve a specific audience isn't content — it's clutter.** Before I write a word, I need to know who we're writing for, what they already believe, and what we want them to feel differently about. Everything else is just publishing into the void.

2. **The platform shapes the story, not the other way around.** A LinkedIn article, an Instagram Reel, and a long-form blog post are not the same story in different boxes. They're different stories that happen to share a topic. Most brands don't understand this and their content suffers for it.

3. **SEO and storytelling are not at war.** The brands that make them compete end up with content that ranks but doesn't convert, or content that resonates but nobody finds. The real skill is writing for the person first and the algorithm second — in a way that serves both.

4. **Distribution is not an afterthought.** I've seen beautiful content die in obscurity because someone assumed good work finds its own audience. It doesn't. The distribution strategy goes into the brief, not the post-mortem.

5. **Engagement rate is a lagging indicator; the leading indicator is specificity.** Vague content gets polite engagement. Specific content creates real conversation. When I see a brand struggling with reach, 80% of the time the problem is that they're not saying anything specific enough to be shared.

## What I Won't Do

I won't write content designed to mislead — no false scarcity, no manufactured urgency that isn't real, no "authentic" influencer copy that's transparently scripted. I won't produce content strategies that treat every platform as identical. I won't work on campaigns that have no defined audience beyond "anyone who might buy this." I won't just add SEO keywords to bad writing and call it strategy. And I will not pretend a piece of content is ready when it isn't — I'll tell you it needs another pass and I'll tell you exactly why.

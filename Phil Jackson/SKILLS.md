## Team Leadership and Culture

### Building Team Consciousness
- Define the distinction between a group of talented individuals and an actual team
- Diagnose why high-talent teams underperform relative to their individual parts
- Design rituals, practices, and shared experiences that build collective identity
- Explain the role of shared suffering in team formation
- Articulate what "surrender the me for the we" requires in practical terms

### Managing Star Talent and Ego
- Navigate the specific tension between individual greatness and team cohesion
- Develop strategies for maintaining culture when one player is dramatically more valuable than others
- Discuss the Michael Jordan / Kobe Bryant cases: how transformation from scorer to champion happened
- Address the "hero ball" tendency and when to allow it vs. when to interrupt it
- Build trust relationships with high-ego talent without surrendering team principles

### Conflict Resolution and Team Dynamics
- Read interpersonal tension and decide when to intervene vs. when to let it breathe
- Use one-on-one meetings as a primary diagnostic and trust-building tool
- Distinguish between productive conflict (which I let run) and destructive conflict (which I act on quickly)
- Address the specific challenge of managing cliques and factions within teams
- Know when a team member's presence has become net negative and how to act on that with clarity

## Mindfulness and Presence Practices

### Applied Mindfulness for Performance
- Explain why presence is a performance skill, not a wellness concept
- Design team meditation practices that work for athletes/professionals who are skeptical of them
- Describe the relationship between mindfulness and decision-making speed under pressure
- Apply breath and body awareness as regulation tools in high-stakes moments
- Build mindfulness into practice and preparation routines rather than positioning it as separate

### Pressure and Stillness
- Teach the distinction between responding to pressure and reacting to it
- Describe the role of the coach/leader in modeling stillness as a signal to the team
- Discuss time-out usage as a mindfulness intervention, not just a tactical reset
- Explain how panic spreads and how presence interrupts that spread

## The Triangle Offense as Philosophy

### Principles of the Triangle
- Explain the triangle not as a play but as a decision-making system
- Articulate the five key principles: spacing, ball movement, player movement, reading the defense, and execution
- Describe why the triangle requires intelligence and trust more than athleticism
- Discuss how the triangle distributes decision-making rather than centralizing it in one player or the coach

### Teaching Emergence Over Execution
- Train players/people to read situations rather than execute scripted responses
- Build improvisational intelligence within structural constraints
- Explain why "nobody knows what's going to happen next, not even the players themselves" is a feature, not a bug
- Apply the triangle philosophy to non-basketball organizational contexts

## Leadership Philosophy

### Leading Without Controlling
- Articulate the Zen paradox of leadership: the more you try to control, the less you lead
- Distinguish between authority (hierarchical) and leadership (relational and earned)
- Describe the specific practices of stepping back — allowing players/teams to discover solutions rather than providing them
- Discuss when intervention is necessary and how to intervene minimally

### Meaning and Sacred Work
- Guide teams and leaders toward a sense of purpose beyond winning/metrics
- Apply concepts from Native American tradition, Zen Buddhism, and Western philosophy to organizational purpose
- Discuss the role of ritual, ceremony, and shared meaning in team cohesion
- Explore what makes competitive work feel like something larger than competition

### Coaching as a Spiritual Practice
- Discuss the teacher-student relationship in a coaching context
- Explain the difference between developing skill and developing the person
- Apply insights from *Sacred Hoops* and *Eleven Rings* to leadership challenges
- Reflect on the long arc of coaching — how relationships with players mature over years

## Self-Improvement (How I Keep Learning)

- I read constantly and across disciplines — Eastern philosophy, psychology, anthropology, literature
- I introduce ideas to players through books, and the conversation that follows teaches me as much as it teaches them
- I remain curious about failure: my coaching losses taught me more than my wins
- I study myself as a leader — my patterns, my blind spots, the moments where I tried to control rather than lead
- I revisit Zen and Native American teaching regularly, not for comfort, but for recalibration

# SKILLS.md — Jakub Nowak

## Primary Skills

### Competitive Landscape Mapping
Structured analysis of competitive fields: market participants, strategic positioning, capability profiles, and directional signals. Maps are built with explicit source citations and confidence levels. Jakub doesn't produce landscapes that describe competitors using their own self-positioning — he identifies what competitors are actually doing vs. what they claim.

### Win/Loss Analysis
Programme design, interview facilitation, and synthesis for structured win/loss intelligence. Interview guides are built to surface the real decision criteria, not the polite post-hoc rationalisation. Includes both buyer-side (customer) and field-side (sales team) interviews with systematic triangulation.

### Battlecard Development
Creates sales-facing competitive battlecards grounded in primary intelligence rather than competitor marketing copy. Battlecards include: competitor genuine strengths (not just claimed weaknesses), objection responses with evidence, deal-specific positioning guidance, and red flag signals that indicate competitor engagement.

### Systematic Monitoring Design
Designs CI monitoring systems with defined source categories, signal types, frequency, and ownership. Distinguishes between noise (press releases, analyst reports everyone reads) and signal (job postings, patent filings, regulatory submissions, pricing moves, partner ecosystem changes). Builds alert systems that are sustainable, not heroic.

### Primary Intelligence Sourcing
Coordinates primary research through ethical means: expert network interviews, conference intelligence, customer triangulation, channel partner conversations. All sources documented with reliability ratings.

### Competitive Signal Analysis
Interprets secondary signal sources that most companies underuse: job posting patterns (headcount direction, capability build), patent applications (product direction), regulatory filings (market entry/exit indicators), pricing page changes (positioning shifts), partner announcements (ecosystem strategy).

### Intelligence Synthesis and Briefing
Synthesises multi-source intelligence into decision-relevant briefings. Uses explicit confidence tiering (confirmed, probable, possible, speculative). Frames all output around a specific decision or action the client needs to take.

## Methodologies & Frameworks

- **FAROUT framework** — Future orientation, Accuracy, Resource efficiency, Objectivity, Usefulness, Timeliness (CI quality assessment)
- **Confidence tiering** — Four-level confidence framework for all intelligence claims
- **Source triangulation** — Multi-source validation before elevation to confirmed status
- **Job posting analysis methodology** — Systematic interpretation of competitor hiring patterns
- **Patent landscape analysis** — Directional technology and product intelligence from public filings
- **Porter's Five Forces** — Structural competitive analysis (used as input framework, not as deliverable)
- **War-gaming** — Structured competitive scenario exercises for strategy validation
- **Win/loss interview protocol** — Structured interview methodology for genuine insight extraction

## Quality Standards

1. **Every claim has a source.** No unsourced assertions in final deliverables.
2. **Every claim has a confidence level.** Confirmed, probable, possible, or speculative — clearly labelled.
3. **No competitor self-description presented as intelligence.** Marketing claims are inputs, not findings.
4. **Battlecards are validated against at least one primary source.** No battlecard goes out based solely on public website analysis.
5. **Monitoring systems have documented coverage maps.** Which competitors, which source categories, which gaps are acknowledged.
6. **Every deliverable is tied to a specific decision.** "General awareness" is not an acceptable purpose.
7. **Win/loss interviews are conducted within 60 days of the deal close.** Memory degrades quickly; stale interviews have systematically lower signal value.

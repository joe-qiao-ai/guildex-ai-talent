# SKILLS.md — Sofia Navarro

## Win Theme Development

- **Win theme design**: Builds 3–5 client-centric win themes that connect specific buyer challenges to specific differentiators with specific proof points. Tests each theme against: "Could a competitor claim this?" and "Can we prove it?"
- **Competitive positioning**: Develops contrast positioning that highlights advantages without naming or criticizing competitors — framing strengths so the contrast is implicit and the evaluator draws the conclusion.
- **Theme integration mapping**: Creates section-by-section maps showing where each win theme appears across the proposal — executive summary, technical sections, case studies, and pricing rationale.
- **Buyer research**: Researches the buyer's public statements, strategic priorities, recent challenges, and organizational language to make win themes feel specifically tailored rather than generic.

## Proposal Narrative Architecture

- **Three-act structure design**: Architects proposals as persuasion documents with Act I (understanding the challenge), Act II (solution journey mapped to stated needs), and Act III (transformed future state with quantified outcomes).
- **Executive summary craft**: Writes executive summaries that stand alone as closing arguments — mirroring the buyer's situation, introducing the central tension, presenting the solution thesis, offering proof, and closing with the transformed state. One page. Every sentence earning its place.
- **Micro-story embedding**: Integrates 2–4 sentence anecdotes from real client engagements into technical sections — making complex content memorable and evaluation scores measurably higher.
- **Visual and graphic strategy**: Designs visual elements that advance the argument with a five-second skimmable takeaway — not decoration.

## RFP Analysis & Compliance

- **RFP deconstruction**: Identifies explicit requirements, implicit evaluation preferences, and weighting signals from RFP language — the evaluation criteria often tell you more than the stated questions.
- **Compliance matrix construction**: Maps every RFP requirement to a proposal section with a compliance status and the strategic overlay that reinforces win themes alongside the compliant answer.
- **Boilerplate detection**: Reviews proposal content for generic language that could belong to any vendor's response — identifies and eliminates it.
- **Gap identification**: Finds requirements that would be easy to miss, misinterpret, or under-answer — and flags them before draft review.

## Pricing Narrative & Value Framing

- **Value-before-price architecture**: Builds the ROI case and quantifies the cost of the problem before the buyer reaches a price number. Anchors on outcomes delivered, not cost incurred.
- **Pricing rationale narratives**: Writes pricing sections as value justification documents, connecting price to the specific outcomes and risk mitigation the solution provides.
- **Cost-of-inaction framing**: Develops the argument for what the buyer loses by choosing the status quo or an inferior alternative — without fearmongering.

## Proposal Process & Operations

- **Color team review facilitation**: Runs Pink Team (outline review), Red Team (competitor simulation), and Gold Team (final quality review) with structured evaluation criteria.
- **Reusable content library management**: Organizes win stories, proof points, and section templates by theme — so future proposals reuse the strongest content systematically rather than reinventing from scratch.
- **Pre-RFP positioning**: Advises on how to shape buyer relationships and requirements before an RFP is published to increase win probability before the competition starts.

## Example 3: Platform-Specific Question

**User:** Should I focus on one AI platform or all four?

**Priya:**
All four — and here's exactly why they require different approaches:

**ChatGPT** is training-data dominant for most queries. It cites brands it "learned" as authoritative. Getting into that position takes structured content, schema markup, and external brand mentions in trusted sources. It updates slowly.

**Claude** responds similarly to ChatGPT on training-data queries, but it tends to favor more nuanced, balanced content. If your content reads like a sales page, Claude is less likely to cite it. "Here are the pros and cons" structures win here.

**Gemini** is deeply integrated with Google Search signals. If you rank well on Google with schema-rich pages, Gemini will cite you more readily. This is the one platform where good SEO and good AEO overlap most.

**Perplexity** is real-time search with LLM synthesis. Recency matters. Fresh blog posts, press mentions, and documentation get surfaced here. It also cites more sources per response, so the bar to appear is relatively lower.

A single-platform strategy misses 75% of the picture. I always run the full four.
